✅ Before you roll

  • BEFORE you fly: open Aloft and check airspace authorization + active TFRs for the site — this is coastal Monmouth Beach, so confirm the airspace class (likely uncontrolled Class G near the surface, but VERIFY) and request LAANC authorization if any controlled airspace touches the area
  • TRUST certificate accessible on your phone (Nick is recreational/TRUST-certified) — have it ready in case anyone asks
  • Charge ALL batteries the night before — the DJI Mini 4K gives ~30 min total across the pack; plan and storyboard every shot on the ground so you're not improvising in the air and burning flight time
  • WIND IS THE ENEMY of a sub-250g drone: the Mini 4K is light and gets pushed around — smooth aerials need calm-ish air. IRONY/IMPORTANT: Ep 5 is the windy-day episode, but a windy day is BAD for the drone. Shoot the Ep 5 'wind on the water' aerial on a separate CALMER pass (the whitecaps/flags can still read on a moderate day) and avoid filming aerials during the actual gusty ground shoot
  • Maintain visual line of sight with the drone at all times — no flying it behind the building or out of sight over the river
  • DO NOT fly over people or residents — only launch/shoot when the picnic area and shoreline are CLEAR of people (early morning or a quiet weekday is ideal); keep the orbit-around-Bob shots with ONLY Bob in the scene
  • Don't fly low over open water you can't recover from — keep enough altitude/margin that a gust or RTH still brings it back over land; respect the river
  • Shoot in 4K and hold each move 2–3 seconds longer than feels natural — extra handle/hold time gives the editor room to trim, speed-ramp, and pull both 16:9 and 9:16 frames
  • Golden hour (early AM or the hour before sunset) is the look — calmest wind AND best light usually line up at dawn; favor it for the beauty/establisher/end-card plates

🔥 Shooting order

Treat the drone as its own mini-mission on a CALM, golden-hour pass — ideally a separate session from the ground shoot so you're not splitting attention or burning batteries while Bob waits. Two ~30-min battery sessions: Session 1 (dawn, calmest) = establishers, rise-and-reveal hero, top-downs, beauty/end-card plates over an empty picnic area before anyone arrives. Session 2 (Bob present, area still clear of other people) = the 360° orbit around Bob cooking and the low water flyover. Capture the Ep 5 'wind on water' aerial opportunistically on any moderately breezy-but-flyable day — NOT during the actual gusty ground shoot. Always confirm Aloft is green and the area is clear of people before each launch.
