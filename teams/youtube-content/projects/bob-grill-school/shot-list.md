## 🔥 Shooting Order — Fire Lifecycle (READ THIS)

A charcoal fire only runs one way: **unlit → lighting → gray-ready → cooking → cooldown.** Plan around **3 fires, not 5 relights.**

**CRITICAL dependency:** You **cannot** demo lighter fluid (Ep 4) on coals already lit by a chimney (Ep 1), and the wind-lighting (Ep 5) also needs an **unlit** firebox. So:

| Fire | Grill | Covers | Blocks |
|------|-------|--------|--------|
| **FIRE 1 — Chimney** | Grill #1 | Ep 1 (full light), then ride the SAME fire into Ep 2 (readiness/hand-test/two-zone), Ep 3 (burgers & dogs), and Ep 5's "re-spin during cook" beat | A(Ep1) → B(Ep1) → C → D |
| **FIRE 2 — Lighter fluid** | Grill #2 (fresh unlit) | All of Ep 4: fluid-on-unlit → side-light → safety beat | A(Ep4) → B(Ep4) |
| **FIRE 3 — Wind** | Grill #3 (fresh unlit, ideally genuinely breezy) | Ep 5 back-to-wind setup + wind-blocked lighting | A(Ep5) → B(Ep5) |

**Batch separately, NO fire needed:** all to-camera hooks/CTAs (**Block E**) and B-roll/end cards (**Block F**). Don't burn a fire on a flat shot.
**Net: 3 fires, ~2 relights.** Pro tip: light **Fire 2 and Fire 3 at adjacent grills back-to-back** to parallelize the dead time.
