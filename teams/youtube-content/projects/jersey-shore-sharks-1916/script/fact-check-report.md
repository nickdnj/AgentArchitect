## Claims That Remain Uncertain

1. **Fisher's last words** -- The script uses "I found the boy. He is on the bottom." This is the version from Capuzzo's *Close to Shore* and widely cited. However, one source (Living Sharks Museum) records his last words as "I did my duty." Both may be apocryphal or represent different moments. The Capuzzo version is the standard one used in documentaries and articles.

2. **Bruder's exact injuries** -- Contemporary newspaper accounts give varying descriptions of which leg was severed above/below the knee. The Matawan Historical Society says "both of his legs appeared to be gone below the knee." Other sources say left above, right below. The script now uses "below the knee" which aligns with the most frequently cited source.

3. **Vansant's age** -- The script now says 23 based on Find a Grave (born Aug 22, 1892). However, many authoritative secondary sources (Smithsonian, National Geographic, Matawan Historical Society) say 25. The "25" may originate from Capuzzo's book or early newspaper accounts. We went with 23 based on the genealogical record.

4. **Bruder's exact age** -- Find a Grave says born 1888, and lists "aged 27-28" at death on July 6, 1916. Without an exact birthday, we cannot confirm whether he was 27 or 28. We used 28, which aligns with the Find a Grave listing and the majority of sources.

5. **Joseph Dunn's age** -- Find a Grave and Wikipedia say 14 (born March 19, 1902). The Matawan Historical Society says 12. Contemporary newspapers listed ages as low as 10. We used 14 based on the genealogical record.

6. **Matawan's distance from the ocean** -- Most commonly cited as 11 miles (Wikipedia, multiple articles). Some sources say 16 miles. The discrepancy may be about measuring from the town center vs. from the creek's tidal mouth. We kept 11 miles as the standard figure.
