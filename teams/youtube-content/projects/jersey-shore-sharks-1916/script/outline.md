# Video Outline: 12 Days of Terror -- The Real Story Behind Jaws

## Target: Documentary thriller, 9 minutes, General YouTube (true crime/history fans, Jaws fans, Jersey Shore locals)

---

## Chapter 1: The Hook (0:00 - 0:45)
### Summary
Open with the Jaws connection, then pull the rug: the real story is worse than the movie. Establish the stakes and pivot to 1916.
### Key Points
- Jaws was not fiction -- it was inspired by real events
- Summer 1916: five shark attacks, four dead, twelve days
- "Before there was Jaws, there was a real monster"
### Scenes: 3
### Visual Approach
Dark, cinematic title card. Moody ocean imagery. Jaws poster silhouette reference (AI-generated, no copyrighted material). Quick cuts to build tension before the title drops.

---

## Chapter 2: The World of 1916 (0:45 - 1:45)
### Summary
Set the stage: a nation gripped by polio and war, fleeing to the beaches. Nobody feared sharks -- science said they were harmless. Introduce the myth of the harmless shark.
### Key Points
- Polio epidemic driving families to the shore
- WWI raging, U-boats in the Atlantic, Wilson at Shadow Lawn
- Hermann Oelrichs' unclaimed $500 reward
- Scientists: "Sharks are harmless to humans"
- Philadelphia Public Ledger headline: "Bathers Need Have No Fear of Sharks"
### Scenes: 2
### Visual Approach
Period beach photos from LOC Detroit Publishing collection. Newspaper headlines. Vintage postcards of Jersey Shore hotels. Ken Burns slow pan across crowded 1910s beach scenes.

---

## Chapter 3: Beach Haven -- The First Blood (1:45 - 2:45)
### Summary
Charles Vansant's fatal attack at the Engleside Hotel -- the first recorded fatal shark attack on the East Coast. And the world's refusal to believe it happened.
### Key Points
- Charles Vansant, 25, swimming before dinner at the Engleside Hotel
- The Chesapeake Bay Retriever
- Shark follows rescuers into ankle-deep water
- Vansant bleeds to death on the hotel manager's desk
- The response: silence. Scientists blame the dog. Life goes on.
### Scenes: 3
### Visual Approach
Engleside Hotel postcard. AI-generated 1916 beach scene. Vansant portrait photo (public domain). Dark, somber tone. Newspaper clippings minimizing the attack.

---

## Chapter 4: Spring Lake -- The Attack That Could Not Be Denied (2:45 - 3:45)
### Summary
Five days later, forty-five miles north: Charles Bruder is attacked with devastating violence in front of hundreds. The scientific establishment scrambles -- and gets it wrong.
### Key Points
- Charles Bruder, 27, Swiss immigrant bellhop at the Essex & Sussex Hotel
- Both legs severed. "A shark bit me! Bit my legs off!"
- Women faint on the beach
- July 8 press conference: scientists blame orcas, sea turtles, "school of turtles"
- Dr. Schauffler alone gets it right -- nobody listens
- Steel nets ordered, but no beach closures
### Scenes: 3
### Visual Approach
Essex & Sussex Hotel HABS photo (LOC). AI-generated ocean attack scene. Museum of Natural History press conference setting. Newspaper headline montage.

---

## Chapter 5: The Warning (3:45 - 4:30)
### Summary
July 12, Matawan Creek. Captain Cottrell sees the shark and tries to warn the town. Nobody listens. The Cassandra moment.
### Key Points
- Temperature: 96 degrees
- Captain Cottrell spots an 8-foot shape from the trolley drawbridge
- Calls the police chief -- dismissed as a joke
- Runs through town shouting -- people laugh
- Matawan Creek is 11 miles from the open ocean
### Scenes: 2
### Visual Approach
AI-generated Matawan Creek scene. Map showing the creek's distance from the ocean. Period Matawan Main Street postcards. Building dread -- music shifts to tension.

---

## Chapter 6: The Creek -- Lester Stillwell (4:30 - 5:30)
### Summary
An 11-year-old boy is taken by a shark in a freshwater creek, miles from the sea. The emotional gut-punch of the documentary.
### Key Points
- Lester Stillwell, 11, one week from his 12th birthday
- Works at the basket factory with his father
- Six boys swimming at Wyckoff Dock
- Ally O'Hara feels "sandpaper" on his leg
- The shark seizes Lester, drags him under
- Boys run through town naked, screaming
### Scenes: 3
### Visual Approach
Stillwell portrait photo (Matawan Historical Society). AI-generated boys swimming in a murky creek. Dark, murky water imagery. This section should feel claustrophobic and unbearable. Slow pacing. Let silence do the work.

---

## Chapter 7: The Creek -- Stanley Fisher, The Hero (5:30 - 6:45)
### Summary
The emotional climax. Stanley Fisher hears the boys screaming, dives into the creek to find Lester's body, and is fatally attacked. His last words on the operating table: "I found the boy. He is on the bottom."
### Key Points
- Stanley Fisher, 24, tailor, choir singer, beloved in Matawan
- Thinks Lester is having an epileptic seizure
- Thirty minutes searching dark water
- Finds Lester's body on the creek bottom
- The shark strikes Fisher -- hip to knee, femoral artery severed
- His last words: "I found the boy. He is on the bottom."
- Fisher dies at 6:45 PM at Monmouth Memorial Hospital
### Scenes: 3
### Visual Approach
Fisher portrait photo. AI-generated murky creek search scene. "Woman with gun at Matawan Creek" photo (public domain). This is the heart of the documentary. Slow, deliberate pacing. Music nearly silent during Fisher's last words. Text overlay for the quote.

---

## Chapter 8: Joseph Dunn and The Catch (6:45 - 7:30)
### Summary
Half a mile downstream, thirty minutes later: Joseph Dunn becomes the sole survivor. Two days later, Michael Schleisser catches the shark.
### Key Points
- Joseph Dunn, 12, from Manhattan, visiting relatives
- Tug-of-war between his brother and the shark
- "I felt my leg going down the shark's throat"
- 59 days in hospital, never speaks publicly about it, dies 1982
- July 14: Schleisser catches a 7.5-foot great white in Raritan Bay
- 15 pounds of human remains inside
- The attacks stop
### Scenes: 3
### Visual Approach
Joseph Dunn portrait. AI-generated brickyard dock scene. Schleisser shark photograph (public domain). Quick pacing -- this section moves fast to contrast with the deliberate Creek section.

---

## Chapter 9: The Aftermath and The Debate (7:30 - 8:15)
### Summary
America declares war on sharks. President Wilson mobilizes the Coast Guard. Scientists still argue about what species did it. The specimen is lost forever.
### Key Points
- Wilson calls a Cabinet meeting; Congress appropriates $5,000
- "U.S. War on Sharks" headline
- Armed mobs with rifles, dynamite, pitchforks
- $250,000 in lost tourism ($7.4 million today)
- Great white vs. bull shark debate
- The taxidermied specimen is lost -- no DNA, no answer, ever
### Scenes: 2
### Visual Approach
Newspaper headlines montage ("U.S. War on Sharks"). Shark hunt photos (Matawan Historical Society). Great white vs. bull shark scientific illustrations. Map showing bull shark freshwater range.

---

## Chapter 10: The Legacy -- Jaws and Beyond (8:15 - 9:15)
### Summary
How twelve days in 1916 created the fear that became Jaws, and how the man who wrote it came to regret what he'd done. Close with the memorial and the graves.
### Key Points
- Before 1916: sharks were harmless. After: man-eating monsters.
- Peter Benchley researched the 1916 attacks
- Jaws (1975) becomes the first summer blockbuster
- The parallels: town, tourism, denial, the hunt
- Benchley's regret: "I couldn't write Jaws today"
- Humans kill 100 million sharks per year; sharks kill 5 humans
- Matawan memorial. Rosehill Cemetery. Fisher and Stillwell buried side by side.
- "The ocean doesn't forget."
### Scenes: 4
### Visual Approach
AI-generated Jaws book imagery (no copyrighted material). Modern Matawan Creek memorial. Rosehill Cemetery gravestones. Quiet, reflective piano. The final image should be the creek, still and silent.
