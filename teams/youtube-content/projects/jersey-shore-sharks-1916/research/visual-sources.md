# Visual Sources: Photos, Maps, and Archival Material

## Key Visual Needs for the Documentary

This document catalogs known visual sources for the 1916 Jersey Shore shark attacks documentary. Since this happened in 1916, **all newspaper content and most photographs from that era are in the public domain** (pre-1926 published works).

   - For Beach Haven and Engleside Hotel images

6. **Find a Grave**
   - Memorials for Charles Bruder, Stanley Fisher, Lester Stillwell, Joseph Dunn
   - May include headstone photos and biographical images

## 3. Maps and Geographic Visuals

### Maps Needed

1. **Overview map of the New Jersey coast** showing all attack locations:
   - Beach Haven (southern LBI) -- July 1
   - Spring Lake -- July 6
   - Matawan Creek -- July 12
   - Raritan Bay (where shark was caught) -- July 14
   - Distances between sites (~70 miles total span)

2. **Detailed map of Matawan Creek** showing:
   - The trolley drawbridge (where Cottrell spotted the shark)
   - Wyckoff Dock (Stillwell and Fisher attack site)
   - Brickyard Dock / NJ Clay Company (Dunn attack site, ~half mile downstream)
   - The railroad trestle (where Stillwell's body was found)
   - Distance from Raritan Bay (~1.5 miles from the mouth)
   - Distance from open ocean (~11 miles)

3. **Map showing the shark's path** (theoretical):
   - Beach Haven to Spring Lake (45 miles north)
   - Spring Lake to Matawan Creek (30+ miles north, then inland)
   - Matawan Creek to Raritan Bay (where caught)

### Map Sources

- **USGS historical topographic maps:** Available free from the National Map (https://ngmdb.usgs.gov/topoview/)
- **Sanborn Fire Insurance Maps (1916 era):** Available through Library of Congress for detailed town layouts
- **Modern satellite imagery** (Google Earth) of Matawan Creek -- the creek still exists and can be visited

## 4. Location Photos (Modern)

### Visitable Sites Today

| Location | What's There Now | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|-------|
| **Beach Haven, LBI** | The Engleside Hotel was torn down during WWII. The site is now a public park honoring military veterans. | Modern Beach Haven beach photos would work for establishing shots |
| **Spring Lake, NJ** | The original Essex & Sussex Hotel is now a luxury condo development (Essex & Sussex). The beach remains. | The building still exists in modified form |
| **Matawan Creek / Wyckoff Dock** | The creek still flows. A memorial plaque stands near the attack site. | From NJ-516/Lower Main St., turn west onto Dock St.; the memorial plaque is in a grassy area on the left |
| **Matawan Main Street Monument** | A memorial stone slab was dedicated July 12, 2016 (100th anniversary) | On Main Street, memorializing the victims |
| **Matawan Railroad Trestle** | A mural depicting the shark attack decorates the railroad trestle visible from Aberdeen Road | Good visual element for the documentary |
| **Rosehill Cemetery, Matawan** | Graves of Stanley Fisher and Lester Stillwell | Visit for headstone photos |
| **Atlantic View Cemetery, Manasquan** | Grave of Charles Bruder | |
