# 1916 Jersey Shore Shark Attacks: Day-by-Day Timeline

## Pre-Attack Context

### 1891
- Millionaire Hermann Oelrichs dives into the ocean alongside a shark to prove they're harmless. He offers a **$500 reward** to anyone who can provide proof of a shark attacking a living person in temperate waters.

### 1906
- Hermann Oelrichs dies (liver disease). His $500 reward has gone unclaimed.

### Early 1916
- **World War I** rages in Europe. German U-boats patrol the Atlantic. America has not yet entered the war but preparedness sentiment is growing.
- **Polio epidemic** begins spreading in New York City. By summer, it will infect 27,000+ Americans and kill 6,000+.

### June 1916
- NYC polio epidemic officially declared. Children warned away from public pools, parks, and beaches. Families flee to the Jersey Shore in search of clean air and relief.
- President Woodrow Wilson establishes his **Summer White House** at Shadow Lawn, a 52-room mansion in West Long Branch, NJ.
- Unprecedented heat wave begins. Water temperatures unusually warm.

---

## The Twelve Days: July 1-12, 1916

### Saturday, July 1 -- ATTACK 1: Beach Haven

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Location** | Beach Haven, Long Beach Island, NJ (southern Jersey Shore) |
| **Victim** | Charles Epting Vansant, 25, Philadelphia |
| **Time** | ~5:30 PM (attack); 6:45 PM (death) |
| **Outcome** | FATAL |

- **5:00 PM:** The Vansant family (Dr. Eugene L. Vansant, son Charles, daughters Eugenia and Louise) arrives at the **Engleside Hotel** in Beach Haven from Philadelphia.
- **~5:30 PM:** While his family unpacks, Charles heads to the beach for a swim before dinner. A Chesapeake Bay Retriever follows him into the water.
- **~5:45 PM:** Vansant begins shouting. Bathers think he's calling the dog. A shark has seized his left leg.
- Lifeguard **Alexander Ott** and bystander **Sheridan Taylor** form a human chain and pull Vansant to shore. The shark follows into shallow water, its belly scraping the sand, before releasing.
- Vansant's left thigh has been stripped of flesh, bone exposed from hip to knee.
- **6:45 PM:** Charles Vansant bleeds to death on the manager's desk in the Engleside Hotel lobby. No ambulance arrives in time.

**Aftermath:** Minimal coverage. *New York Times* reports Vansant "was badly bitten in the surf ... by a fish, presumably a shark." PA Fish Commissioner James M. Meehan suggests the shark was chasing the dog, not Vansant. Beach activities continue normally.

---

### Sunday, July 2 - Wednesday, July 5

- No attacks.
- Limited newspaper coverage of Vansant's death.
- Scientists are skeptical a shark was involved. Some suggest sea turtles or other creatures.
- Hotels and resorts make no changes.
- The *Philadelphia Public Ledger* runs the headline: **"Bathers Need Have No Fear of Sharks."**

---

### Thursday, July 6 -- ATTACK 2: Spring Lake

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Location** | Spring Lake, NJ (45 miles north of Beach Haven) |
| **Victim** | Charles Bruder, 27, Swiss immigrant, bell captain at Essex & Sussex Hotel |
| **Time** | ~2:30 PM |
| **Outcome** | FATAL |

- **~2:00 PM:** Bruder, on his regular afternoon break, enters the water for a swim. He is a strong swimmer and ventures approximately **130 yards (120 meters)** from shore.
- **~2:30 PM:** A shark attacks with devastating force, biting his abdomen and severing both legs -- left above the knee, right below the knee.
- The water turns red with blood visible from shore.
- Lifeguards **Chris Anderson** and **George White** row out in a lifeboat.
- Bruder cries out: **"A shark bit me! Bit my legs off!"** -- then loses consciousness.
- Bruder dies from blood loss before the lifeboat reaches shore.
- Scene on the beach: *New York Times* reports "women [were] panic-stricken [and fainted] as [Bruder's] mutilated body ... [was] brought ashore."
- Hotel guests and workers raise money for Bruder's mother in Switzerland.

**Aftermath:** This attack cannot be denied. Panic begins spreading.

---

### Friday, July 7

- Major newspapers begin front-page coverage. *New York Times* details Bruder's injuries.
- Resort towns begin ordering steel wire mesh nets to enclose bathing areas.

---

### Saturday, July 8

- **Press conference at the American Museum of Natural History** in New York City.
  - Panelists: **Frederic Augustus Lucas** (director), **John Treadwell Nichols** (assistant curator of fishes, the only ichthyologist), **Robert Cushman Murphy**.
  - Nichols suspects the attacker was an **orca (killer whale)**, not a shark.
  - The three scientists stress a third attack is "unlikely" but admit surprise sharks attacked at all.
  - Nichols warns swimmers to stay close to shore and use netted areas.
- Dr. **William G. Schauffler** declares: "There is not the slightest doubt that a man-eating shark inflicted the injuries." He is largely ignored.

---

### Sunday, July 9 - Tuesday, July 11

- Steel nets begin to be installed at beaches.
- Motor boat patrols established in some areas.
- Media coverage intensifies. Newspapers from coast to coast cover the story.
- Theories circulate: sea turtles, orcas, "a shark trained by the Germans."
- A letter to the *New York Times* blames German U-boat activity for driving sharks to shore.
- Shark sightings (real and imagined) reported up and down the coast.
- Coast Guard issues a statement that "so many sharks [spotted along the shore] that a patrol of cutters would be useless."
- Tourism begins to decline but most people still believe the ocean is safe.

---

### Wednesday, July 12 -- ATTACKS 3, 4, AND 5: Matawan Creek

**Temperature: 96 degrees Fahrenheit**

| Time | Event |
|------|-------|
| **6:30 AM** | Lester Stillwell and his father leave for work at the Anderson Basket Factory. |
| **Morning** | Coast Guard issues statement: "so many sharks [spotted along the shore] that a patrol of cutters would be useless." |
| **1:30 PM** | Retired sea captain **Thomas Cottrell** spots an 8-foot dark gray shape moving west up Matawan Creek with the incoming tide from the trolley drawbridge. Four workmen witness it too. |
| **~1:35 PM** | Cottrell calls **John Mulsoff** (town barber and police chief) at his barbershop. Mulsoff dismisses it as a joke. |
| **~1:40 PM** | A motorboat of teens (**Harold Conover, Ralph Gall, John Tassini**, all 18) also spots the shark in the creek. |
| **1:45 PM** | Lester's father lets him leave work early due to the heat. |
| **~1:50 PM** | Cottrell runs through town, past Fisher's shop on Main Street, shouting warnings. People are unconvinced. |
| **~1:55 PM** | The teen motorboat warns Lester's swimming group at Wyckoff Dock about the shark. The boys think it's a joke. |

#### ATTACK 3: Lester Stillwell

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Location** | Wyckoff Dock, Matawan Creek (~11 miles inland from the open ocean) |
| **Victim** | Lester Stillwell, 11 (one week from his 12th birthday) |
| **Time** | ~2:05 PM |
| **Outcome** | FATAL |

- **~2:00 PM:** Boys swimming at Wyckoff Dock: **Lester Stillwell, Albert "Ally" O'Hara** (Lester's best friend), **Charles Van Brunt, Johnson Cartan, Frank Close, Anthony Bubblin**.
- Ally O'Hara feels a **"sandpaper-like object graze his leg."**
- Boys see what looks like "an old, black weather-beaten board or a weathered log" in the dark water.
- **~2:05 PM:** Anthony Bubblin executes a backflip off the dock. At that moment, the shark seizes Lester Stillwell.
- Lester is dragged underwater, surfaces screaming, then pulled under again.
- Boys flee the creek naked and mud-covered, running through the streets screaming.

#### ATTACK 4: Stanley Fisher

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Location** | Wyckoff Dock, Matawan Creek (same site) |
| **Victim** | Watson Stanley Fisher, 24, dry cleaner/tailor, Matawan |
| **Time** | ~2:10 PM (attack); 6:45 PM (death) |
| **Outcome** | FATAL |

- **~2:07 PM:** The boys run past **Fisher's dry cleaning shop** on Main Street, screaming about a shark. Fisher initially suspects Lester is having an **epileptic seizure** (Lester suffered from tonic-clonic seizures).
- Fisher recruits **Red Burlew** and **Arthur Smith** to help search.
- **~2:10 PM:** Three men dive into the murky creek. For ~30 minutes they search the dark, silty water.
- Arthur Smith is scraped on the abdomen by the shark -- draws blood, leaves a permanent scar.
- **~2:40 PM:** Fisher makes one final dive in waist-deep water. He finds Lester's body on the bottom, grabs his leg, begins pulling him up.
- Witnesses briefly see Lester's body surface -- then the shark strikes Fisher.
- The shark tears Fisher's right thigh from hip to knee, removing **at least 10 pounds of flesh** and severing his femoral artery.
- Fisher fights the shark; it drags him under twice.
- Deputy **Arthur Van Buskirk** strikes the shark with an oar, forcing it to release Fisher.
- **Dr. George C. Reynolds** treats Fisher's femoral wound at the scene.
- **5:06 PM:** Fisher placed on a train to Long Branch.
- **5:30 PM:** Fisher enters surgery at Monmouth Memorial Hospital. Dr. **Edwin Field** operates.
- **6:45 PM:** Stanley Fisher dies from blood loss and shock.

#### ATTACK 5: Joseph Dunn

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Location** | Brickyard Dock (NJ Clay Company), ~half mile downstream from Wyckoff Dock |
| **Victim** | Joseph Dunn, 12 (some sources say 14), of upper Manhattan, NYC |
| **Time** | ~2:35 PM |
| **Outcome** | SURVIVED |

- **~2:35 PM:** Joseph, his brother **Michael Dunn** (14), and friend **Jerry Hourihan** are swimming when someone shouts about a shark.
- Joseph is farthest from the ladder. As he climbs out, the shark seizes his left leg.
- A brutal tug-of-war ensues: Michael and Jerry pulling Joseph's arms, the shark pulling his leg.
- Brickyard supervisor **Robert Thress** rushes in and helps pull Joseph free.
- Fisherman **Jacob Lefferts** also jumps in to assist.
- Joseph is severely injured but alive. Captain Cottrell transports him to the Wyckoff dock area, then to **St. Peter's Hospital** in New Brunswick.
- Dr. **H.S. Cooley** treats Joseph Dunn.

---

### Thursday, July 13

- Matawan authorities dynamite the creek in an attempt to kill the shark. Unsuccessful.
- Mayor **Arris B. Henderson** posts a **$100 reward** for killing the shark.
- National media descend on Matawan. Story is front-page news across the country.
- **Massive shark hunt** begins along the NJ and NY coasts. Citizens arm themselves with rifles, harpoons, pitchforks, spears, and dynamite.
- President Wilson considers federal action.

---

### Friday, July 14

- **5:30 AM:** Train conductor **Harry Van Cleaf** discovers **Lester Stillwell's body** floating near the railroad trestle, approximately 150 feet west of Wyckoff Dock.
- **Governor James Fielder** "virtually mandates" steel wire mesh enclose all popular swimming locations.
- Fisher's parents arrive by train.
- **Michael Schleisser** and **John Murphy**, fishing in Raritan Bay about 4 miles from the mouth of Matawan Creek, catch a **7.5-foot, 325-pound shark** in their nets. The shark attacks their boat. Schleisser kills it with a broken oar handle.
- Schleisser cuts open the shark's belly and finds **"suspicious fleshy material and bones"** -- about two-thirds of a milk crate, weighing 15 pounds.
- **Dr. Frederic Lucas** examines the remains: "They are the parts of the left radius and ulna [lower arm bones] and one of the anterior left ribs, all human. There is no doubt about this."
- The shark is identified as a **juvenile great white**.
- Schleisser mounts and taxidermies the shark, displays it in a Broadway shop window in Manhattan.

---

### Saturday, July 15

- Separate funeral services for **Stanley Fisher** and **Lester Stillwell**.
- Both are interred at **Rosehill Cemetery**, Matawan.

---

## Post-Attack Timeline

### Mid-July 1916
- President Wilson calls a **Cabinet meeting** to address the shark crisis.
- Treasury Secretary **William Gibbs McAdoo** proposes mobilizing the Coast Guard.
- Wilson orders Coast Guard cutters to patrol the shore. *Washington Post* headline: **"U.S. War on Sharks."**
- **Congress appropriates $5,000** ($150,000 in 2025 dollars) for shark eradication.
- Hundreds of sharks killed in hunts along the coast. Few if any contain human remains.
- No further shark attacks occur.

### August-September 1916
- Tourism losses estimated at **$250,000** ($7.4 million in 2025 dollars).
- Sunbathing declines **75%** at some beaches.
- **September 2:** Wilson delivers his re-election acceptance speech from Shadow Lawn.
- **September 15:** Joseph Dunn released from St. Peter's Hospital after 59 days.

### July 1918
- Fisher family commissions a **stained glass window** in the Matawan Methodist Church using $7,500 from a life insurance policy. The window is damaged by a local blast five months later.

### 1970s
- Methodist Church demolished. The Fisher stained glass window is auctioned to a private collector. Its current location is unknown.

### 1974
- **Peter Benchley** publishes *Jaws*.

### 1975
- **Steven Spielberg's *Jaws*** released, becoming the first summer blockbuster.

### April 1982
- **Joseph Dunn**, the sole survivor, dies at age 79. He lived a quiet life, rarely discussing the attack.

### July 12, 2016
- **100th anniversary** commemorations in Matawan. Memorial dedicated on Main Street.
