## Cross-Reference Map

### Monmouth Beach ↔ Seven Presidents Park
- **Life-Saving Service**: Monmouth Beach's 1895 station is part of the same coastal network that served Long Branch. Reference: "20 miles north, another station stood watch."
- **Wreckers/Wreckmasters**: The wrecker mythology in Monmouth Beach applies to the entire Monmouth County coast including Long Branch.
- **Millionaire's Row**: Monmouth Beach had its own version; Long Branch's Ocean Avenue was the original. Cross-promote: "If you watched our Monmouth Beach video, you know the Shore had more than one Millionaire's Row."
- **Rum Runners**: Sea Bright skiffs mentioned in Monmouth Beach video were built in the Long Branch area.
- **George Robeson**: The corrupt Navy Secretary from Monmouth Beach was a founder of the Monmouth Beach Association AND connected to Long Branch elite circles.

### Monmouth Beach ↔ 1916 Shark Attacks
- **Geography**: Monmouth Beach is between Long Branch and Sea Bright — the shark swam past this exact coastline.
- **Timeline**: The attacks happened in 1916. Monmouth Beach was still a functioning resort community at this time.
- **Cross-promote**: End screen link from shark video → Monmouth Beach: "Want to know more about this coastline? Watch our Monmouth Beach documentary."

### Seven Presidents Park ↔ 1916 Shark Attacks
- **Woodrow Wilson**: Wilson was at Shadow Lawn in Long Branch during the July 1916 shark attacks. He was president when it happened. This is a DIRECT narrative connection.
- **Cross-promote**: In the shark video, mention: "The President was just a few miles away at his summer White House." In the Seven Presidents video, mention: "Wilson's summer at Shadow Lawn in 1916 was interrupted by something no one expected — a series of shark attacks that terrorized the Shore."
- **Long Branch setting**: Both videos share the same geographic backdrop.

### Seven Presidents Park ↔ Bahr's Landing
- **Geography**: Highlands (Bahr's Landing) is at the tip of Sandy Hook, directly north of Long Branch/Monmouth Beach.
- **Resort era**: Both were part of the Jersey Shore's golden age of tourism.

### Monmouth Beach ↔ Sea Bright (future)
- **Twin towns**: Literally adjacent on the same barrier beach between ocean and Shrewsbury River.
- **Sea Bright skiff**: The famous boat design mentioned in the Monmouth Beach rum runners segment originated in Sea Bright.
- **Shared infrastructure**: Same seawall, same flooding problems, same Hurricane Sandy devastation.
- **Cross-promote**: "Right next door to Monmouth Beach, Sea Bright has its own story — and its own legendary boat."

### 1916 Shark Attacks ↔ Sea Bright (future)
- **Sea Bright skiff**: The same boats used by rum runners and fishermen would have been in the water during the shark attacks — Sea Bright fishermen may have been part of the shark hunt.

## Cross-Promotion Strategy

### In-Video Mentions
- Brief, natural callbacks: "If you've seen our Monmouth Beach video, you know..."
- NOT forced plugs — these should feel like the narrator connecting dots
- Place them where the story naturally touches the other topic

### YouTube Cards
- Add at the exact moment another video is referenced
- Keep to 1-2 cards per video max

### End Screens
- Always include: Subscribe button + next video recommendation
- Recommend the most narratively connected video, not just the newest

### Playlists
- "Local History — Jersey Shore" — all NJ videos in chronological order of the events (not upload order)
- "The Monmouth County Story" — Monmouth Beach, Seven Presidents, Sea Bright, shark attacks
- "Presidential History" — Seven Presidents overview + 7 deep dives

### Description Links
- Every video description should link to related videos:
  ```
  Related Vistter videos:
  • Monmouth Beach: Sand, Scandal, and the Shore — [URL]
  • Seven Presidents Park — [URL]
  ```
