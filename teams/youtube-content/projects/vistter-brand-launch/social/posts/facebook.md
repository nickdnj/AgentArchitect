## Historic Long Branch Group

### Pre-Premiere Post (T-2 | Thursday, March 26)

> This is one of my favorite Gilded Age stories from this area, and I bet most people here haven't heard it:
>
> In 1871, eighteen of the most powerful men on the East Coast -- including the Secretary of the Navy, a Supreme Court Chief Justice, and a U.S. Senator -- formed the Monmouth Beach Association and bought up the entire coastline.
>
> They built what locals called "The Wooden Palaces" along a 1.6-mile stretch of Ocean Avenue. The street was 100 feet wide and nicknamed "The Golden Road."
>
> Then Congress discovered that $15 million in Navy construction funds had vanished, and two of the Association's founders were at the center of the scandal.
>
> Anyone know which mansions from that era are still standing (in any form)?

### Premiere Day Post (T-0 | Saturday, March 28)

> Quick question for the group -- how many of you know the history of the Sea Bright skiff?
>
> During Prohibition, the flat-bottomed fishing skiffs that Sea Bright and Monmouth Beach fishermen had been using for generations were modified into something new: souped-up speedboats designed to outrun Coast Guard cutters.
>
> On the darkest nights, rum runners would race into the shallows, beach the skiffs, hand off crates of whiskey to a waiting truck, and vanish before the law arrived.
>
