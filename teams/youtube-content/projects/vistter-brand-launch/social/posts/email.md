This one covers 12,000 years of a town most people have never heard of -- a 1.6-mile strip of sand on the Jersey Shore with a history that includes:

- **Land pirates:** For generations, locals were accused of luring ships onto the rocks with false lights. There's no evidence it ever happened.

- **Storm Warriors:** After a shipwreck killed 240 immigrants off Asbury Park, the government built the U.S. Life-Saving Service. Their oath: *"You have to go out. Nothing says you have to come back."*

- **Millionaire's Row:** Eighteen of the most powerful men in America bought the coastline and built "Wooden Palaces." Then Congress discovered the Secretary of the Navy had embezzled $15 million.

- **Rum runners:** During Prohibition, local fishermen turned their flat-bottomed skiffs into speedboats that could outrun Coast Guard cutters.

- **Hurricane Sandy:** In one night, millions of cubic yards of sand were swept away. The ocean and the river met -- with people in between.

The Life-Saving Station from 1895 still stands. Monmouth Beach endures.
