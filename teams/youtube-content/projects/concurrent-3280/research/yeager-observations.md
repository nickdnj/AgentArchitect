# Ken Yeager — observational notes from the 3280 lab, Christmas 1985

Source: Nick DeMarco, Concurrent Computer co-op, Tinton Falls NJ, December 1985. First office job — observational details only, no firsthand technical interaction.

**Use:** texture for the storyboard — humanize Yeager without making the story about Nick. These are details a quiet kid in the corner of the lab would have noticed.

## The man

- **Rode a bicycle to work every day** — mid-1980s, suburban NJ. Almost nobody did that.
- **Cuffs rolled up at the ankles** — likely from the bike commute, never let down.
- **Checkered shirts** — bowling-shirt style, plain cut. Decent jeans. Same rotation.
- **Hummed constantly** at his desk. Not anything recognizable. Just hum, head down, working.
- **Mostly invisible** — stayed in his office. Came out, rode home, came back.

## The work pattern (as observed from outside)

- Engineers had **audiences** with Yeager — go in, come out with direction.
- Yeager handed out **scraps of paper** with handwritten notes — instructions, sketches, decisions.
- Each engineer would take their scrap and execute on it.
- Co-ops never went in. The architecture happened on those scraps.

## The phone list anchor

- Mike Martone posted the **3280 lab phone list** in the ex-Concurrent Facebook group (~40 years after the fact).
- Nick's name is on that list as a co-op.
- This is the only personal-connection moment we use in the video. No narration, no first-person voiceover — just the artifact onscreen, the docent's name visible in the column, and the audience can decide what to make of it.

## Tone guidance

- Yeager's death (Aug 2017) is stated factually. No sentimentality.
- The bike, the scraps, the humming — these go in as observational B-roll-style narration, not as personal memoir.
- The story is about the machine and the architect. Nick is the docent who found the phone list, nothing more.
