# 📱 Shooting Guide — Comdyna GP-6 Short

**For:** the docent / volunteer shooting at the museum
**Gear:** a phone is fine. A small tripod (or a steady surface) is the one thing that matters most.
**Goal:** capture 6 short clips of the GP-6 "flying a ball" so we can drop them into the **locked ~55-second cut** and publish.

> **Status (2026-06-22): the previz is LOCKED.** Script, Nick's narration, music, and timing are final. These 6 clips simply **replace the AI stand-ins** — match each shot number to its scene. The graphic scenes (the Apollo-era beat, the InfoAge website card, the end card) are already done — **you do not shoot those.** Capture a few seconds *longer* than you think for each; trimming is easy.

---

## Before you start (2 minutes)

1. **Power on** the GP-6 and the HP 1200B oscilloscope.
2. **Patch it for the projectile demo** and put the machine in **Repetitive Operation (RO)** — follow `museum/comdyna-gp6/04-OPERATOR-SETUP-CARD.md`. You want a **stable green arc (parabola)** redrawing on the scope.
3. **Quick check:** if the scope is flat or off-screen, the machine is probably in IC/HOLD, or the GRAVITY / INITIAL VELOCITY knob is turned all the way down. Confirm RO/Operate and nudge the knobs up.

## Universal rules (apply to every clip)

- 📱 **Shoot VERTICAL** (phone upright). Tall, not wide.
- 🎚️ Lock to **1080p or 4K, 30fps**. Tap the screen to **lock focus + exposure** on the subject so it doesn't hunt.
- 🧱 **Tripod or braced.** Steady beats fancy. No walking shots.
- 🐢 **Move slowly.** Turn knobs *slowly* — the magic is watching the curve morph; fast twists just look like noise.
- 🔁 Shoot **2–3 takes** of each. Storage is free; a second trip is not.
- 🔇 Don't worry about talking/room noise — we replace the audio with narration.
- 🌳 Reduce screen glare if you can (dim nearby lights, angle the scope away from windows).

## The 6 shots (in priority order)

### 🥇 #1 — THE MONEY SHOT (do this one first, get it right)
**Both the GRAVITY knob and the scope's arc in the SAME frame, in one continuous take.**

- Frame so the **GRAVITY knob (Pot #2)** is in the lower part of the frame and the **green parabola on the scope** is in the upper part. Both visible at once.
- Roll the camera, then **slowly turn the GRAVITY knob DOWN** — watch the arc **stretch out** (that's "the Moon"). Hold a beat.
- Then **slowly turn it back UP past normal** — the arc **collapses / drops fast**. Hold a beat.
- **Do not cut, do not move the camera.** The whole point is seeing the curve change live as the hand turns the knob. ~15–20 seconds of continuous footage. Shoot it 3 times.

### 🥈 #2 — Scope close-up (the cold open)
- Tight on the **scope screen only** — the glowing green parabola pulsing / redrawing.
- Get close enough to see the scan-lines. Hold steady ~8–10 seconds.

### 🥉 #3 — Front-panel beauty shot
- The **whole GP-6 front panel**: the tangle of red/black patch cords, the knobs, the red voltmeter.
- Straight-on, well-lit. A very slow push-in or a static hold both work. ~8 seconds.

### #4 — Patch-cord macro (hands)
- Close-up of a **hand pushing a patch cord into a jack**, then resting along the cords.
- Get in tight — we want texture. ~6–8 seconds.

### #5 — Voltmeter + knob macro
- Close-up of the **red voltmeter readout** (it'll show something like −0.5xx), then a **hand turning a knob**.
- Two quick beats, ~4 seconds each.

### #6 — Wide gallery / context shot
- Step back: the **GP-6 + scope as it sits on the floor at InfoAge**, with the room visible.
- This proves it's a real place people can walk into. ~6 seconds, steady.

### ⭐ Nice-to-have (only if time)
- The **printed placard** beside the machine (1 quick clip).
- **Velocity view:** if you can switch the scope to show the *falling straight line crossing zero* (that's the top of the flight — apogee), grab ~6 seconds. Bonus payoff shot.
