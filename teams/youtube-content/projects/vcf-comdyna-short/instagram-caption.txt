This 1970s box solves this equation faster than your laptop. ⚡

No chip. No code. No software. It's a Comdyna GP-6 — an analog computer. You wire it up with patch cords, turn knobs, and the machine *becomes* the equation.

Here it's "flying a ball" — solving projectile motion live on an oscilloscope. Turn gravity down and the arc stretches out (that's the Moon 🌙). Crank it up, it slams down. Nothing re-runs — the wiring IS the program. It's how engineers flew Apollo and guided missiles for 30 years.

See it running at the VCF Museum @ InfoAge — Wall, NJ, on the old Army Signal Corps base where they helped invent the radar that won WWII. Run by volunteers. Open weekends. You've driven right past the sign. 📍
