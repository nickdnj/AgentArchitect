## 1. Concept & Hook Rationale

This one converts on **provocation**, not nostalgia. The hook is a flat, confident, slightly outrageous claim — *"This 1970s box solves this equation faster than your laptop"* — laid over a green parabola breathing on an oscilloscope. It is engineered to trigger the "akshually" reflex: every CS major, every gamer, every benchmark nerd in the feed will want to dunk on it in the comments ("a modern CPU does billions of FLOPS, this is nonsense"). That argument **is the product** — sustained comment threads are pure algorithm fuel, and the claim is *defensible*, so the debate never resolves and the engagement never stops.

The payoff is the myth-bust the SGI short trained the audience to expect: there's **no chip, no code, no software, no 1s and 0s** — you program it with **patch cords and knobs**, and it doesn't *calculate* the ball's flight, it **becomes** it. The dopamine centerpiece is a single unbroken shot of a hand turning the **GRAVITY knob** while the parabola reshapes **live, continuously, with no re-run** — visual proof of the "faster at this" claim, because there is no clock to wait on. Then we collapse the whole story into the local punch — *"it's in Wall, New Jersey, you've driven past the sign"* — and one frictionless CTA: **come turn the knob yourself.** Motion does the persuading throughout; we never sit on a static hardware photo.

**Why "faster" is true (and how we keep it honest):** for this *class* of problem — a continuous differential equation, solved in real time — the analog box answers **instantly and in parallel**, because the wiring *is* the equation; there's no clock, no time-slicing, no loop. A digital machine must chop the flight into thousands of discrete steps and grind through each one. So the box genuinely responds faster *at this*. It is NOT faster at everything — digital won on precision, memory, and flexibility, and we say so in the honesty note (do-not-voice) below so the creator never overclaims on camera.

> **Honesty note for the creator (do NOT voice):** The claim "faster than your laptop" is true *only for this class of problem* — a continuous differential equation solved in real time.
