# Script Review Brief — VCF Comdyna GP-6 Short (for Codex)

You are a sharp short-form video script editor (YouTube Shorts / Reels / TikTok). Critique and improve the narration script below. Be specific and ruthless about retention.

## Goal
A ~45–52s vertical short that drives **physical visits** to the VCF Museum @ InfoAge (Wall, NJ) and teaches a hit of computer history. Hook is debate-bait: *"This 1970s box solves this equation faster than your laptop."* The payoff: it's an **analog computer** — no chip, no code; you "program" it with patch cords + knobs, and it doesn't *calculate* a ball's flight, it *becomes* it. Centerpiece = turning the GRAVITY knob and the parabola on the scope reshapes live ("that's the Moon").

## Hard constraints
- **Honesty:** "faster" is true ONLY for this class of problem (a continuous ODE solved in real time, in parallel, no clock/time-steps). NOT faster in general — digital won on precision/flexibility. Do not let the script overclaim.
- Keep the line: *"It doesn't calculate the motion — it becomes the motion."*
- The CTA is a single low-friction ask: **come turn the knob yourself.**

## What I specifically want from you
1. Tighten the hook — does the first 2 seconds earn the swipe-stop? Propose 2–3 alternate opening lines.
2. Pacing/retention: where does it sag? What to cut to hit ~45s without losing the payoff?
3. **InfoAge presence:** the client wants MORE about InfoAge near the end (scenes 7–8) — it's the actual destination (former Camp Evans / Fort Monmouth Signal Corps base, volunteer-run computer + tech museum). Weave in 1 concrete, intriguing InfoAge detail that rewards a visit, without bloating the runtime.
4. Flag anything technically wrong or that a smart commenter would dunk on.
