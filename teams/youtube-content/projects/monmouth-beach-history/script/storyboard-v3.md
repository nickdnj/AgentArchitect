## Scene 3d: The Treacherous Coast **[NEW - V3, UPDATED - V8]**
- **Segment:** 3 (Land Pirates)
- **Duration:** 10 seconds
- **Image source:** Historical map — `images/maps/1879-noaa-coastal-chart-sandy-hook-to-barnegat.jpg`
- **Map details:** 1879 NOAA/Coast Survey nautical navigation chart, "Sandy Hook to Barnegat." Shows the coast from Sandy Hook south with depth soundings, sandbars, and coastal hazards marked throughout. Clean black and white engraving, no bureaucratic stamps. Visually explains exactly why ships wrecked here — the shallow, treacherous approach is legible at a glance.
- **Image orientation:** portrait (pillarbox with blurred background, or slow vertical pan)
- **Text overlay:** "Coast Survey, 1879 — Sandy Hook to Barnegat: A Mariner's Nightmare"
- **Motion:** ken-burns-pan (slow vertical pan from Sandy Hook down the coastline to the Monmouth Beach area)
- **Audio:** Narration continues — underscore how the geography made shipwrecks inevitable and life-saving stations essential
- **Narrative purpose:** Bridges from the wrecker era to the Life-Saving Service. The nautical chart's depth soundings and sandbars make it immediately clear why this coast was deadly to shipping — no bureaucratic noise, just the raw geography of disaster.
- **Transition out:** Crossfade 1.5s

- **Segment:** 5 (Millionaire's Row)
- **Duration:** 12 seconds

- **Map details:** c.1900 Rutgers University property lot survey, titled "Monmouth Beach Property." Ultra-wide panoramic map (14,340px) showing the full length of the Monmouth Beach barrier peninsula. Individual property lots are plotted along Ocean Avenue between the Atlantic Ocean (south) and Shrewsbury River (north), with owner names inscribed on each parcel. The narrow strip of land — barely a few blocks wide — is divided into dozens of estates.
- **Image orientation:** ultra-wide panoramic (will need slow horizontal scroll)

- **Audio:** Narration continues — describe how every inch of this narrow sandbar was claimed by the wealthy
- **Narrative purpose:** This is the documentary's "money shot" for the Millionaire's Row segment. The map proves the narrative — you can see every estate lot with the actual names of the Gilded Age owners who carved up this narrow barrier island. The Atlantic Ocean on one side, the Shrewsbury River on the other, and nothing but wealth in between.

## Scene 8c: The Insurance Map **[NEW - V3]**
- **Segment:** 8 (Galilee and the Fishermen)
- **Duration:** 10 seconds
- **Image source:** Historical map — `images/maps/1890-sanborn-fire-insurance-nj-coast-index.jpg`
- **Map details:** 1890 Sanborn-Perris Fire Insurance Map, "Insurance Maps of the New Jersey Coast" index page (Library of Congress). Three-panel index showing the mapped coverage areas: Atlantic Highlands and Sandy Hook (top left), the full barrier peninsula from Sandy Hook south through Monmouth Beach (right panel), and the Monmouth Beach/South Shrewsbury detail (bottom left). Every building footprint, property boundary, and structure type documented for fire insurance purposes.
- **Image orientation:** portrait
- **Text overlay:** "Sanborn Fire Insurance Map, 1890 — Every Building Documented"
- **Motion:** ken-burns-zoom (zoom into the Monmouth Beach detail panel at bottom left, showing the individual structures)
- **Audio:** Narration continues — by 1890, Monmouth Beach had grown enough that insurance companies mapped every structure. The fishing village and the mansions, side by side on the same narrow strip of sand.
- **Narrative purpose:** Closes the Galilee segment by showing the contrast between two worlds coexisting on the same peninsula — the fishermen's village and the millionaires' estates, all meticulously documented by the insurance surveyors. Transitions the narrative toward the 20th century.
- **Transition out:** Crossfade 1.5s
