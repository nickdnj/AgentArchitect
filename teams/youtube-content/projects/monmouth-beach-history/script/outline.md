# Video Outline: Monmouth Beach -- Sand, Scandal, and the Shore

## Target: Documentary, 6-7 minutes, general audience / NJ history buffs

## Research Summary

### Key Themes Identified
1. **Ancient origins** -- Lenape habitation dating back 12,000 years; land purchased for 4 pounds
2. **The Wreckers** -- Monmouth County's "land pirates" accused of luring ships to destruction with false lights; later vindicated
3. **Gilded Age excess** -- "Millionaire's Row" on Ocean Avenue; the Monmouth Beach Association's 1871 power grab by senators, judges, and a scandal-plagued Navy Secretary
4. **Presidential connections** -- Seven presidents summered nearby; Benjamin Harrison stayed in Monmouth Beach
5. **George Robeson scandal** -- Co-founder of the Monmouth Beach Association, Secretary of the Navy under Grant, accused of embezzling $15 million; narrowly avoided impeachment
6. **The Galilee fishermen** -- Scandinavian commercial fishing village supplying New York markets
7. **Life-Saving Service** -- The "Storm Warriors" who risked everything; motto: "You have to go out, but nothing says you have to come back"
8. **Prohibition rum-running** -- Modified Sea Bright skiffs outrunning Coast Guard cutters; Monmouth County's inlets as bootlegger highways
9. **The ocean's wrath** -- Shipwrecks, hurricanes, and Hurricane Sandy's devastation; one of the most heavily engineered coastlines in the world
10. **Modern Monmouth Beach** -- Population 3,200, median income $140k, median home ~$750k; the quiet aftermath of a dramatic past

### Notable Figures
- **Eliakim Wardell** -- Purchased the land from the Lenape in 1688 for 4 pounds
- **George M. Robeson** -- Secretary of the Navy, co-founder of Monmouth Beach Association, scandal-plagued
- **Alexander Cattell** -- US Senator, co-founder, voted to impeach Andrew Johnson, business partner in Robeson scandal
- **George Fisher Baker** -- "Most powerful commercial banker in US history," built "Seaverge" mansion, endowed Harvard Business School
- **Louis James** -- "Greatest Shakespearean actor on the American stage," mansion burned 1924
- **Dr. Edward Lawrence Keyes** -- Son of Civil War general, pioneering urologist
- **Benjamin Harrison** -- 23rd President, summer guest in the 1890s

## Hook (0:00 - 0:20)
Open with the accusation: the people of this shore were called "land pirates" -- accused of luring ships to their doom with false lights. The truth is more complicated, and far more interesting.

## Section 1: Before the Name (0:20 - 1:30)
- Lenape habitation for 12,000 years along the Shrewsbury River
- Seasonal fishing, shellfish, hunting, and trading
- 1688: Eliakim Wardell buys 454 acres for 4 pounds in blankets and hardware
- The Lenape population collapse: 7,000 in 1600, fewer than 60 by the Revolution
- Visual: atmospheric Shrewsbury River, Lenape settlement, colonial transaction

## Section 2: Land Pirates and Storm Warriors (1:30 - 3:00)
- The "wreckers" reputation: accused of using false lights to lure ships onto rocks
- 1799 New Jersey law criminalizing false wrecking lights
- The reality: wreck-masters were government-appointed rescuers, not pirates
- The John Minturn disaster and the founding of the US Life-Saving Service
- Monmouth Beach Life-Saving Station built 1895 (now the Cultural Center)
- The surfmen's oath: "You have to go out, but nothing says you have to come back"
- Visual: stormy seas, shipwrecks, heroic rescue scenes, life-saving station

## Section 3: Millionaire's Row (3:00 - 4:30)
- 1871: The Monmouth Beach Association -- 18 wealthy men buy up the coast
- The founders: a Navy Secretary, a Supreme Court Chief Justice, a US Senator
- George Robeson's scandal: $15 million missing from the Navy, a $320,000 "gift" cottage
- Alexander Cattell's dual role: voted to impeach one president, defrauded another's administration
- "Uncommon exclusiveness" -- the 1878 New York Times description
- George Fisher Baker's "Seaverge" and the rise of Ocean Avenue's wooden palaces
- Visual: grand Victorian mansions, Gilded Age portraits, Ocean Avenue panorama

## Section 4: Bootleggers and Fishermen (4:30 - 5:45)
- Galilee: the Scandinavian fishing village that fed New York
- The Galilee Fishing Association chartered 1884
- Prohibition transforms the coast: experienced seamen become rum-runners
- Modified Sea Bright skiffs souped up as speedboats
- Cat-and-mouse with Coast Guard cutters in the dark
- The extension of the Rum Line to 12 miles -- the beginning of the end
- Visual: fishing village, boats at dock, nighttime smuggling, Coast Guard pursuit

## Section 5: The Ocean Always Wins (5:45 - 6:30)
- The Flottbek shipwreck of 1901
- The 1931 seawall destruction
- One of the most heavily engineered coastlines in the world
- Hurricane Sandy, 2012: ocean and river meet with people in between
- Millions of cubic yards of sand swept away
- A storm of historic magnitude
- Visual: storm damage, seawall construction, Sandy aftermath, aerial views

## Conclusion (6:30 - 7:00)
- Today: 3,200 residents, ~$750k median home price, median age 58
- The quiet aftermath of centuries of drama
- The Life-Saving Station still stands as the Cultural Center
- Monmouth Beach endures -- battered but never broken
- Visual: modern beach scenes, sunset over Shrewsbury River, Cultural Center
