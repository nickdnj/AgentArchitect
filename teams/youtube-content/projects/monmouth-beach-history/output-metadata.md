## Description

They called them land pirates — men who used false lanterns to lure ships onto the rocks, then plundered the wreckage. The residents of Monmouth Beach, New Jersey carried that accusation for generations. But the truth is far more complicated — and far more fascinating.

This documentary traces the full, unvarnished history of one of New Jersey's most overlooked shore towns: from the Lenape who fished its rivers for centuries, to the wreckers whose reputation may have been unfairly written by their enemies, to the heroic Storm Warriors of the U.S. Life-Saving Service who risked everything to pull sailors from drowning ships. From the Gilded Age mansions of Millionaire's Row to a $15 million Navy corruption scandal, from a working-class fishing village to Prohibition rum-runners racing through the surf — Monmouth Beach has seen it all.

And then Hurricane Sandy took most of it away.
