# Photo Research Report: Batter Up Documentary
## Real Historical Photos to Replace AI-Generated Imagery

**Research Date:** 2026-03-18
**Researcher:** Web Research Agent
**Purpose:** Locate authentic historical photographs for use in "Batter Up: The Last Piece of Jolly Roger's"

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This research identified **six primary source categories** containing real historical photographs. The single most important action item is **contacting Robert Berkowitz directly** — he is the photographer of record for the pre-demolition 1979 shots and is reachable at RSBImageWorks@aol.com. His photos are available for purchase and would be the most powerful visuals in the documentary.

The second most actionable find is the **Plainedge Public Library** photo gallery with 15 items including opening-day photographs from 1951, a postcard of the Jolly Roger ride, and a portrait of William Nunley himself. These are held by a public library and may be licensable at low or no cost.

There are **no confirmed Creative Commons or public domain photographs** of Happyland or Jolly Roger specifically — all identified photos are either under copyright (Robert Berkowitz) or held by institutions (Plainedge Library, Nassau County Archives) requiring direct contact.

1. **Email Robert Berkowitz at RSBImageWorks@aol.com** — explain you're producing a YouTube documentary about the Batter Up site's history as the last piece of Jolly Roger's.
