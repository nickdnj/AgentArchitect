# Video Outline: Why Are We Building Data Centers in Space?

## Target: Documentary, 13-14 minutes, tech-savvy YouTube audience

## Chapter 1: The Hook (0:00 - 0:40)
### Summary
Cold open that establishes the absurdity — one GPU in orbit attracted $170M in funding, and the company filed for 88,000 satellites. Set up the central question.
### Key Points
- Starcloud put a single H100 GPU in orbit (Dec 2025), raised $170M at $1.1B valuation
- Filed with FCC for 88,000-satellite constellation (Feb 2026)
- That would be 9x the size of Starlink — for computing, not internet
### Scenes: 3
### Visual Approach
Earth from orbit, dramatic text reveals, cinematic split title card (space vs. ocean)

## Chapter 2: The Space Pitch (0:40 - 2:35)
### Summary
Steelman the space data center argument fairly — solar efficiency, cooling advantages, the companies involved, the grand Dyson sphere vision. Build the case before dismantling it.
### Key Points
- Solar 5x more efficient in orbit (no clouds, no night in sun-sync orbit)
- Radiative cooling eliminates water for cooling
- Starcloud ($200M), Orbital (a16z, Apr 2027 test), Thales (2036 concept), Google/NVIDIA (exploratory)
- Long-term Dyson sphere vision
### Scenes: 3
### Visual Approach
Aspirational satellite renderings, clean company cards with funding stats, epic orbital complex concept art

## Chapter 3: The Math That Kills It (2:35 - 4:55)
### Summary
Systematically dismantle the space case with four devastating data comparisons: capital cost, lifespan, total cost of ownership, and latency.
### Key Points
- 10MW space DC: $1.6-11B capital, 2-5 year lifespan, zero maintenance
- 10MW ocean DC: $160-530M capital, 25-30 year lifespan, ROV maintenance ($30K/day)
- 25-year TCO: space $25B+ vs. ocean $3-4B
- Latency: space 20-50ms (kills AI inference) vs. ocean +0.65ms (negligible)
### Scenes: 4
### Visual Approach
Clean data visualizations, bar charts, timeline cost curves, latency path diagrams — let the numbers speak

## Chapter 4: The Ocean Is Already Working (4:55 - 7:50)
### Summary
The proof section — real companies with real hardware in real water. Microsoft Natick proved the technology, and multiple companies are now operational or deploying prototypes.
### Key Points
- Microsoft Natick: 864 servers, 35m deep, 2 years, 8x lower failure rate, cancelled for business (not tech) reasons
- Highlander/HiCloud (China): operational in Hainan, wind-powered demo near Shanghai
- Aikido Technologies: Norway prototype 2026, NVIDIA partner, UK commercial 2028
- Nautilus Data: 7MW floating DC in Stockton, 86% occupied
- Hyperscale Data: ran DC on Gulf of Mexico oil platform (Nov 2024 - Feb 2025)
- NetworkOcean (YC W24): 1MW capsule in SF Bay test
- Subsea Cloud: multiple projects (WA, GoM, North Sea)
### Scenes: 4
### Visual Approach
World map with glowing operational dots, underwater capsule renderings, documentary-style imagery

## Chapter 5: The Infrastructure Is Already There (7:50 - 10:15)
### Summary
The "we already do harder things" chapter — submarine cables carry 99% of traffic, 12,000 platforms await repurposing, flared gas is wasted energy, offshore wind is already at scale.
### Key Points
- 1.5 million km submarine cable, 99% of international data traffic
- 12,000 offshore platforms, many end-of-life
- UK: $56B decommissioning budget; Gulf of Mexico: $40-70B
- Platforms already have power, cranes, helipads, seawater cooling
- 151 BCM gas flared annually = enough to power every US data center
- Crusoe Energy: 250+ MW deployed, 63% CO2 reduction, proven model
- 84.5 GW offshore wind installed globally
### Scenes: 4
### Visual Approach
Submarine cable network map, aging North Sea platform imagery, satellite gas flare imagery, sweeping offshore wind farm aerials

## Chapter 6: The Environmental Case (10:15 - 12:20)
### Summary
The moral argument — land DCs drain aquifers in water-stressed regions, space DCs would destroy the ozone and seed Kessler syndrome, ocean DCs use zero freshwater and create marine habitats.
### Key Points
- US DCs: 66 billion liters direct water use (2023), 2/3 in water-stressed areas
- Ocean DCs: zero freshwater, ocean is the heat sink
- Falcon 9: ~425 tonnes CO2, black carbon 500x more warming in stratosphere
- ETH Zurich 2025: launch rates will slow ozone recovery
- 100% e-waste on satellite reentry, zero recovery
- 54,000 tracked orbital objects, CRASH clock at 2.8 days
- Platform reefs: 12,000-14,000 fish per structure, Rigs-to-Reefs program
### Scenes: 4
### Visual Approach
Desert DC vs. ocean split, rocket exhaust, orbital debris visualization, beautiful underwater reef ecosystems

## Chapter 7: The Verdict (12:20 - 14:20)
### Summary
Land the argument — dismiss the Dyson sphere as a thought experiment vs. working infrastructure, expose the funding gap absurdity, paint the real opportunity, and close with quiet confidence.
### Key Points
- "A Dyson sphere is not a roadmap — it's a thought experiment"
- $200M = 1 GPU in orbit vs. multiple MW-scale operational ocean DCs
- Infrastructure, power, cooling all exist today — not in 2036
- The answer is right there, and it is a little strange we are looking up
### Scenes: 5 (including end card)
### Visual Approach
Dyson sphere dissolving into real wind turbine, funding gap infographic, cinematic ocean montage, peaceful golden-hour closing shot
