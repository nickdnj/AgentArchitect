# Production Notes

## Word Count
- Total narration: ~2,520 words
- Estimated runtime at 150 wpm: ~16 minutes 48 seconds
- With pauses, transitions, and title card: ~17 minutes 20 seconds

## Workforce Story Integration
The workforce narrative appears in three distinct moments:
1. **Scene 3 (0:30-0:50)** — EARLY: Plants the seed during the cold open. 70,000 UK jobs lost, 95,000 Gulf jobs lost. Connects the two stories before the viewer knows where this is going.
2. **Scenes 18-19 (9:40-11:30)** — MIDDLE: Full workforce section woven into the infrastructure chapter. The skills match, the worker quotes, the 90% transferability finding. Shows the workforce already exists.
3. **Scenes 28-30 (15:25-17:00)** — LATE: The emotional payoff in the closing chapter. Inner space vs outer space is not just about cost or physics — it is about whether we use our people or discard them. The community multiplier, the pay gap honesty, the final argument outer space cannot make.

## "Inner Space" Language Usage
The "inner space" / "outer space" framing appears in:
- Title card (Scene 4)
- Chapter titles (Chapters 2, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8)
- Cost comparison labels (Scenes 8, 9, 10, 11)
- Submarine cable section (Scene 16) — "the backbone of the internet is in inner space"
- Platform decommissioning (Scene 17) — "inner space DC sites vs outer space ones"
- Reef section (Scene 25) — "in inner space"
- Closing argument (Scene 30) — "every argument for outer space works better in inner space"
- Final line (Scene 31) — "inner space has been waiting"
- Funding gap (Scene 27) — "inner space is not a concept, it is a business"
