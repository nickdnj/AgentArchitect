## Production Notes

### Vertical crop strategy

The source video is 16:9. The documentary uses Ken Burns pan/zoom effects on still images. For vertical cropping:

- **Segments 1 and 5 (disputed presidents):** Center crop works. Most images are portrait-oriented or centered compositions.
- **Segment 4 (Garfield/railroad):** The Frank Leslie's engravings are wide horizontal compositions. Use a slight left-weighted crop to capture the action in the torchlight scene -- the workers are centered-to-right. Use `crop=1080:1920:(iw-1080)/2:0` and verify the key subject is visible.
- **Segment 6 (Wilson/Shadow Lawn):** The Shadow Lawn photograph is wide. Center crop captures the mansion facade well.

### Subtitle style

Pop-style subtitles, burned in via ASS:
- Font: Arial Black, 88pt
- Color: White with 4px black outline
- Highlight color: Yellow for key proper nouns (Grant, Garfield, Wilson, Shadow Lawn, Elberon) and numbers (7, 4, 3,200, 3,773)
- Position: Center of screen, MarginV 380 (avoids hook text top, avoids CTA bottom)
- 2-5 words per phrase, phrase-by-phrase pacing

### Assembly script

See `assemble-shorts.py` in this directory. Run each phase independently:
1. `python assemble-shorts.py extract` -- extract raw clips from source
2. `python assemble-shorts.py crop` -- convert to vertical 9:16
3. `python assemble-shorts.py overlay` -- add hook text + CTA text
4. `python assemble-shorts.py transcribe` -- Whisper transcription for subtitles (requires Whisper at localhost:8880)
5. `python assemble-shorts.py subtitles` -- generate ASS files from transcription JSON
6. `python assemble-shorts.py burn` -- burn subtitles into final videos
7. `python assemble-shorts.py thumbnails` -- extract thumbnail frames

Or run all phases: `python assemble-shorts.py all`
