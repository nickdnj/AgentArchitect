# Seven Presidents Documentary Series -- Proposed Structure

## 8-Video Series: 1 Overview + 7 Deep Dives

---

## Video 1: OVERVIEW
### "Seven Presidents Park: When the Jersey Shore Was America's Summer Capital"

**Target Length:** 15-20 minutes
**Tone:** Epic, sweeping, inviting

**Structure:**

**Cold Open (1-2 min):**
- Modern footage of Seven Presidents Park -- beach, waves, families
- Voiceover: "This beach has a secret. Right here, where kids build sandcastles and surfers catch waves, seven American presidents once walked the sand."
- Quick montage of the seven presidents with dates
- Title card

**Act 1: The Rise (4-5 min)**
- How Long Branch became a resort (1780s origins, steamboat era)
- Mary Todd Lincoln's 1861 visit
- The railroads arrive (1860s-1870s)
- Grant receives his cottage (1869) -- Long Branch becomes the "Summer Capital"
- Lewis B. Brown develops Elberon -- "Playland of Millionaires"

**Act 2: The Golden Age (4-5 min)**
- The grand hotels: Continental, Elberon, Hollywood
- The social scene: Diamond Jim Brady, Lillian Russell, Lily Langtry
- Phil Daly and the "Monte Carlo of America" gambling era
- Monmouth Park Racetrack and Grant in his box
- McKim, Mead & White designing the mansions
- The Church of the Presidents

**Act 3: The Presidents (4-5 min)**
- Quick portrait of each president's connection (teasing the deep dives):
  - Grant: the cottage, the Summer White House
  - Hayes: the disputed visit (tease the controversy)
  - Garfield: shot in D.C., died at Long Branch (the biggest story)
  - Arthur: the accidental president who came to mourn
  - Harrison: the Cape May president with an Elberon connection
  - McKinley: the flag speech and the dying VP
  - Wilson: Shadow Lawn and "He Kept Us Out of War"

**Act 4: The Decline and Legacy (3-4 min)**
- Why Long Branch faded: gambling ban, automobiles, Atlantic City
- The 1987 pier fire
- Pier Village and the modern renaissance
- The Garfield Tea House -- built from railroad ties
- The Church of the Presidents restoration
- Seven Presidents Park: how we remember

**Closing (1 min):**
- "Seven presidents walked this beach. Their stories are America's stories."
- Tease the seven deep-dive videos

## Video 2: GRANT
### "Grant's Summer White House: The President Who Put Long Branch on the Map"

**Target Length:** 12-15 minutes
**Tone:** Grand, personal, bittersweet

**Structure:**

**Cold Open:** Julia Grant's memoir quote about the cottage

**Act 1: The General Arrives (3-4 min)**
- Grant visits Long Branch in 1867 as Commanding General
- George Childs's invitation and friendship
- The clever gift: "presenting it to Mrs. Grant"
- The $40,000 cottage at 995 Ocean Avenue

**Act 2: The Summer Capital (4-5 min)**
- The Summer White House operations: papers, messengers, cabinet meetings
- Daily routine: carriage rides, poker Fridays, swimming in the Atlantic
- Julia's perspective from her memoirs
- Grant as Long Branch's "first citizen"
- The social circle: Childs, Pullman, Gould
- Monmouth Park racetrack

**Act 3: The Fall (3-4 min)**
- The Grant & Ward scandal (1884): financial ruin at the shore
- Writing the memoirs at Long Branch -- from president to pauper to author
- The throat cancer diagnosis at the cottage
- Grant's death in 1885

**Closing:** The cottage's decline, the 1963 demolition, the empty lot today

---

## Video 3: HAYES
### "The Phantom President: Did Hayes Ever Actually Visit Long Branch?"

**Target Length:** 8-10 minutes
**Tone:** Investigative, surprising, myth-busting

**Structure:**

**Cold Open:** "Every monument says he was here. But was he?"

**Act 1: The Claim (2-3 min)**
- What the park brochures say
- What the church plaques say
- The traditional narrative of Hayes at Long Branch

**Act 2: The Investigation (3-4 min)**
- The Monmouth Timeline research
- Hayes's diary: summers at the Soldiers' Home
- No newspaper accounts, no hotel records
- The 1925 financial crisis at the Church of the Presidents
- Ernest Levy, Bernard Sandler, and the creation of the "six presidents" claim
- "The Westminster Abbey of America"

**Act 3: The Real Hayes (2-3 min)**
- The Compromise of 1877 and the end of Reconstruction
- "Rutherfraud B. Hayes" and "Lemonade Lucy"
- Why he might NOT have fit in at Long Branch

**Closing:** How history gets made -- and remade

---

## Video 4: GARFIELD
### "79 Days: The Assassination and Death of President Garfield at Long Branch"

**Target Length:** 18-25 minutes (the longest and richest episode)
**Tone:** Dramatic, tragic, cinematic

**Structure:**

**Cold Open:** "On a small street in Elberon, New Jersey, there's a granite marker between a road and a garage. Most people drive past without noticing. It marks the spot where an American president died."

**Act 1: The Dark Horse (3-4 min)**
- Garfield's remarkable rise: canal boy to president
- The 36th ballot at the convention
- The Stalwart-Half Breed divide
- Arthur as the Stalwart balance on the ticket

**Act 2: The Assassin (3-4 min)**
- Charles Guiteau: the Oneida Community, "Charles Gitout"
- The failed newspaper, the failed law career
- The delusional belief he won the election for Garfield
- The demand for the Paris consulship
- Blaine's explosive refusal
- The ivory-handled revolver ("it would look better in a museum")
- Stalking the president

**Act 3: The Shooting (3-4 min)**
- July 2, 1881: the Baltimore and Potomac Railroad Station
- Two shots from behind
- "My God, what is this?"
- "I am a Stalwart of the Stalwarts! Arthur is now President!"

**Act 4: The Doctors (3-4 min)**
- The parade of unsterilized fingers and instruments
- Dr. "Doctor" Willard Bliss and his stubbornness
- The 20-inch incision
- Alexander Graham Bell's metal detector
  - The induction balance device
  - Tests on Civil War veterans
  - The metal mattress springs and Bliss's refusal to check the left side
  - The autopsy revelation: the bullet was on the other side

**Act 5: The Journey to Long Branch (3-4 min)**
- The decision to move Garfield to the shore
- September 5: the overnight railroad
  - 3,200 feet of track laid by torchlight
  - Sandwiches from the Elberon Hotel
  - Lemonade for the workers
  - The crowd pushing the train around the bend by hand
- "Thank God, it is good to be here."
- The Francklyn Cottage: Charles G. Francklyn, the Cunard Line, McKim's design

**Act 6: Death (2-3 min)**
- Thirteen days at the shore
- September 19, 1881: 10:35 PM
- Arthur's 2:15 AM swearing-in
- The journey to Elberon with Blaine and Lincoln's son
- "The meeting between Mrs. Garfield and Mr. Arthur is described as an affecting one."

**Closing:** The legacy
- Guiteau's trial and execution ("I am going to the Lordy")
- The Pendleton Civil Service Reform Act of 1883
- The Garfield Tea House: a memorial made of railroad ties
- The granite marker: an 8-year-old boy's campaign in 1957
- "The doctors killed Garfield; Guiteau just shot him."

## Video 5: ARTHUR
### "The Accidental President: How Garfield's Death Changed Chester Arthur"

**Target Length:** 10-12 minutes
**Tone:** Reflective, redemptive, nuanced

**Structure:**

**Cold Open:** "No one expected Chester Arthur to be a good president. Least of all Chester Arthur."

**Act 1: The Machine Politician (3-4 min)**
- Arthur's rise through the New York patronage system
- Roscoe Conkling's protege
- The Port Collector of New York -- and his removal by Hayes
- The Stalwart faction and the VP nomination

**Act 2: The Shadow of Assassination (3-4 min)**
- Guiteau's cry: "Arthur is now President!"
- The 79-day vigil: Arthur in impossible limbo
- The night of September 19-20
- The journey to Elberon: visiting Garfield's widow
- The affecting meeting -- with Blaine and Robert Todd Lincoln at his side

**Act 3: The Transformation (3-4 min)**
- The Stalwart who became a reformer
- Signing the Pendleton Civil Service Reform Act
- The Tiffany White House redecoration
- The 80 pairs of pants
- The secret illness (Bright's disease)
- One term, one transformation

**Closing:** Arthur's 1884 summer at Sea Bright, near where Garfield died. The weight of the place. His death in 1886.

## Video 6: HARRISON
### "The Cape May President: Benjamin Harrison's Jersey Shore Summers"

**Target Length:** 10-12 minutes
**Tone:** Political, personal, questioning

**Structure:**

**Cold Open:** The Wanamaker cottage scandal

**Act 1: The Grandson President (3-4 min)**
- William Henry Harrison's 31-day presidency (1841)
- Benjamin Harrison's rise: lawyer, general, senator, president
- The 1888 election: winning without the popular vote

**Act 2: The Jersey Shore Summers (3-4 min)**
- Congress Hall as the Summer White House (1891)
- Wanamaker's $10,000 gift to "Mrs. Harrison"
- The scandal in Washington
- Cape May Point and Cape May summers
- Caroline Harrison's death in the White House (1892)

**Act 3: The Long Branch Question (2-3 min)**
- The thin evidence for Harrison at Elberon
- The 1898 Elberon Club dinner
- The 1927 newspaper claims
- How Harrison became the "seventh president"
- The rivalry between Cape May and Long Branch for presidential prestige

**Closing:** The seven presidents completed -- and what we choose to remember

## Video 7: McKINLEY
### "The Flag and the Fallen: William McKinley's Visit to a Dying Friend"

**Target Length:** 10-12 minutes
**Tone:** Patriotic, somber, foreshadowing

**Structure:**

**Cold Open:** McKinley's flag speech quote at Ocean Grove

**Act 1: The Spanish-American War (3-4 min)**
- The war of 1898
- America as a new imperial power
- The debate over expansion
- McKinley's political position

**Act 2: The Visit (3-4 min)**
- August 25, 1899: the presidential parade through Asbury Park
- The Ocean Grove speech at the Great Auditorium
- "Wherever that flag is raised, it stands not for despotism but for liberty"
- The visit to VP Hobart at Normanhurst in West Long Branch
- Hobart's Bright's disease and decline
- Hobart's death in November 1899

**Act 3: The Second Assassination (3-4 min)**
- The vacancy that put Theodore Roosevelt on the ticket
- September 6, 1901: Leon Czolgosz at the Pan-American Exposition
- McKinley's death on September 14
- The parallel with Garfield: two presidents connected to Long Branch, both assassinated
- The national mourning

**Closing:** The end of the Gilded Age; the beginning of the Progressive Era

---

## Video 8: WILSON
### "Shadow Lawn: The Last Summer White House on the Jersey Shore"

**Target Length:** 12-15 minutes
**Tone:** Political, dramatic, reflective

**Structure:**

**Cold Open:** Wilson on the steps of Shadow Lawn, September 2, 1916

**Act 1: Wilson's New Jersey (3-4 min)**
- Princeton student, professor, university president
- Governor of New Jersey (1910-1912)
- The Sea Girt nomination (1912)
- The only New Jersey native among the Seven Presidents (though Cleveland was born in NJ, he's not on the list)

**Act 2: Shadow Lawn (4-5 min)**
- The 52-room mansion's history
- Wilson arrives September 1, 1916
- The acceptance speech: September 2
- The "front porch" campaign from the beach
- "He Kept Us Out of War" -- a slogan that was "more of a hope than a promise"
- Six weeks at the Summer White House
- The 1916 election: Hughes going to bed thinking he won; California's 3,773 votes

**Act 3: The Aftermath (3-4 min)**
- Wilson leaves Shadow Lawn November 9, 1916 -- never returns
- April 1917: asking Congress for war -- the broken promise
- The 1927 fire: the mansion burns, visible for 20 miles
- The replacement: Horace Trumbauer and Julian Abele
- Monmouth University acquires the campus
- The 2020 renaming: "The Great Hall at Shadow Lawn" -- Wilson's name removed for his racism

**Closing:**
- Wilson was the last president to use the Long Branch area as a summer capital
- The end of an era that began with Grant in 1869 and ended with Wilson in 1916
- Today: Seven Presidents Park, a public beach where anyone can visit
- The democracy of the shore: from exclusive presidential retreat to public park

## Series Production Notes

### Consistent Elements Across All Videos
- **Opening title sequence:** Waves at Seven Presidents Park + "Seven Presidents" title treatment
- **Map graphics:** Recurring map of Long Branch/Elberon showing presidential locations
- **Timeline graphics:** Consistent timeline bar showing the 1869-1916 presidential era
- **Closing sequence:** Return to the park; the Garfield monument; ocean views

### Recommended Release Order
1. Overview (hook the audience)
2. Grant (set the foundation)
3. Garfield (the peak drama -- this is the viral episode)
4. Arthur (follows naturally from Garfield)
5. Hayes (the mystery/controversy)
6. Harrison (the Cape May question)
7. McKinley (builds toward the end)
8. Wilson (closing the era)

### Cross-Reference Opportunities
- The Garfield episode should reference Grant (who may have snubbed him) and Arthur (who succeeded him)
- The Arthur episode should reference Garfield extensively
- The McKinley episode should reference Garfield (parallel assassinations)
- The Wilson episode should reference Grant (bookend: first and last summer capitals)
- The Hayes episode can reference the overview's "seven presidents" mythology

### Total Estimated Series Length
- Overview: 15-20 min
- 7 Deep dives: 8-25 min each (avg ~12 min)
- **Total: approximately 100-105 minutes of content**

### Potential Thumbnail Concepts
1. Overview: Seven silhouettes against an ocean backdrop
2. Grant: Split image -- the cottage / empty lot today
3. Hayes: "WAS HE REALLY HERE?" text over the park monument
4. Garfield: Railroad tracks leading to the ocean at night (dramatic)
5. Arthur: A man in a top hat standing alone at the shore
6. Harrison: Split: Congress Hall (Cape May) vs. Seven Presidents Park
7. McKinley: American flag + ocean + "1899" date
8. Wilson: Shadow Lawn mansion, dramatic lighting
