# Visual Sources for Seven Presidents Documentary Series

## Where to Find Historical Photos, Maps, and Images

---

## 1. Library of Congress (Primary Source -- Public Domain)

The Library of Congress holds extensive documentation of Long Branch's presidential history through the **Historic American Buildings Survey (HABS)** and other collections.

### HABS Collections

**Ulysses S. Grant Cottage (HABS NJ-884)**
- Multiple photographs from 1933 (before demolition in 1963)
- Exterior views, interior details, porch views, demolition photos
- URL: https://www.loc.gov/resource/hhh.nj0884.photos?st=gallery
- Specific demolition photo: https://www.loc.gov/resource/hhh.nj0884.photos/?sp=17
- Main record: https://www.loc.gov/item/nj0884/

**Shadow Lawn / Wilson's Summer White House (HABS NJ-1188)**
- Photographs from 1933 of the estate
- URL: https://www.loc.gov/resource/hhh.nj1488.photos

**The Reservation Site Map (HABS NJ-883)**
- Nate Salsbury's Buffalo Bill-connected cottage colony
- New Ocean & Joline Avenues, Long Branch
- URL: https://www.loc.gov/item/nj0883/

**Town of Long Branch (HABS NJ-1289)**
- General views from 1933
- State Route 36, South of Shrewsbury River
- URL: https://www.loc.gov/item/nj1289/

### Other LOC Collections

**Postcards and Prints:**
- "Shadow Lawn, Elberon -- Wilson's Summer White House" (postcard, c. 1916): https://www.loc.gov/item/2014700071/
- Hallway interior of Shadow Lawn: https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/2014702148/
- Elberon Hotel, Long Branch (c. 1900s): https://www.loc.gov/item/2016808797/

**Baltimore & Potomac Railroad Station (Garfield Assassination Site):**
- Photo of the station where Garfield was shot: https://www.loc.gov/item/96516095/

### LOC Search Tips
- Search "Long Branch" + "New Jersey" in the Prints and Photographs Division
- Search "Elberon" for additional materials
- HABS/HAER collections are fully digitized and in the public domain

## 3. Monmouth County Historical Association (MCHA)

**Research Library & Archives:**
- One of the largest collections of local published and unpublished materials in New Jersey
- More than 1,000 manuscript collections documenting 300+ years of Monmouth County life
- **Access:** By appointment only
- **Website:** https://www.monmouthhistory.org/visit-library
- **Contact for research inquiries:** Use their online contact form

**What to look for:**
- Period photographs of Long Branch hotels and mansions
- Maps of the Elberon development
- Photographs of the Church of the Presidents
- Images of Monmouth Park Racetrack (1870s-1890s)
- Any Garfield-related materials

## 8. Modern Location Photography (B-Roll Ideas)

### Seven Presidents Oceanfront Park
- The park itself -- beach, boardwalk, Skateplex
- Garfield statue and bronze tablets at end of Laird Street
- Wide ocean views from the park
- Sunrise/sunset shots over the Atlantic

### Church of the Presidents
- 1260 Ocean Avenue, Long Branch
- Carpenter Gothic architecture
- Garfield Tea House on the grounds
- Presidential plaques and memorials inside (when accessible)
- Note: Building undergoing restoration; check access before visit

### Garfield Death Site
- Garfield Road off Ocean Avenue, Elberon
- The granite marker
- The residential setting -- poignant contrast between a presidential death site and a suburban driveway

### Shadow Lawn / Monmouth University
- The Great Hall at Shadow Lawn (the 1929 replacement building)
- Beaux-Arts architecture by Horace Trumbauer / Julian Abele
- Campus grounds where Wilson gave his acceptance speech
- Interior tours may be available

### Elberon Neighborhood
- Ocean Avenue -- the "Millionaire's Row" of the 1870s-1900s
- Modern mansions mixed with historical context
- The site of Grant's cottage (995 Ocean Avenue) -- now a grassy field/new construction
- Elberon train station area (where the emergency Garfield rail spur began)

### Ocean Grove
- The Great Auditorium (where McKinley spoke in 1899)
- Victorian architecture of the camp-meeting community
- The auditorium interior

### Pier Village / Modern Long Branch
- The phoenix from the ashes of the 1987 pier fire
- Modern restaurants and shops on the old pier site
- Contrast shots: old Long Branch vs. new Long Branch

## 9. Maps and Engravings

### Period Maps
- **F.W. Beers 1860 Map of Monmouth County** -- available digitized through Long Branch Library
- **HABS Site Maps** -- available through Library of Congress
- **Sanborn Insurance Maps** -- available through LOC or ProQuest (check for Long Branch coverage)

### Engravings and Illustrations
- 19th-century newspapers (Harper's Weekly, Frank Leslie's Illustrated) frequently published engravings of Long Branch scenes
- **LOC Chronicling America** newspaper archive may contain illustrated coverage of Garfield's death
- Search: https://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/

## Rights and Usage Notes

- **Library of Congress HABS photos:** Public domain, free to use
- **Library Company of Philadelphia:** Check individual image rights
- **Monmouth County Historical Association:** Likely requires permission and possibly fees
- **Stock footage (Critical Past):** Licensed; requires purchase
- **Modern location photography:** Original photography is free to use
- **Flickr images:** Check individual Creative Commons licenses
- **Newspaper archives:** Generally public domain for pre-1928 publications
