William Shockley had not been in the lab on December twenty-third. He had been traveling. When he got back and learned what Bardeen and Brattain had done without him, he was, by every account, furious. He tried to get his name added to the patent application — alone. Bell Labs' lawyers looked at Shockley's own writings, found them uncomfortably close to a nineteen-twenty-five patent by a German physicist named Julius Lilienfeld, and quietly left Shockley's name off the document entirely.
