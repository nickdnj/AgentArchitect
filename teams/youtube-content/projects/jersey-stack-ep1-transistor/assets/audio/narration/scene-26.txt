Eleven years later, in nineteen sixty-eight, two of those eight — Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore — left Fairchild and started Intel. Eugene Kleiner co-founded Kleiner Perkins, the venture firm that funded most of what came next. The others seeded AMD, National Semiconductor, and dozens more. Silicon Valley, the place and the culture, did not originate in California. It was a diaspora. The seed that Shockley carried west was germanium dust from a basement laboratory in Murray Hill, New Jersey. Silicon Valley came from Jersey. It just built a better brand.
