You can still walk onto a piece of that story today. The old Signal Corps radar base at Camp Evans, in Wall Township, is now home to InfoAge — a volunteer-run campus of museums on the original ground where the Army once tracked enemy aircraft and bounced radar off the moon. Inside one of those museums — the Vintage Computer Federation, or VCF — sit examples of the earliest transistor-era technology, alongside the story of how the Signal Corps helped move transistor research out of Bell Labs and into real-world military and communications use. Same soil. Same story. Different century. Fort Monmouth didn't just help launch the transistor. Fort Monmouth is about to invent something of its own. But that is the story of another episode.
