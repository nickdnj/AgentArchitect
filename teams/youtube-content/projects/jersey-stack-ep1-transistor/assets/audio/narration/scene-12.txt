Walter Brattain was Bardeen's opposite — loud, funny, raised on a ranch in Washington State. Brattain had what Bell Labs people called "the best pair of hands in the building." He could assemble an experiment that no one else could get to work. While Bardeen reasoned about it on a chalkboard, Brattain built it on a bench. The two of them, it turned out, were perfectly paired. They shared an office.
