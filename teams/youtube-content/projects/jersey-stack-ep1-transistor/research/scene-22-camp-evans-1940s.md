## What Was NOT Found

- **Aerial/exterior view of Camp Evans buildings, 1940s:** No digitized aerial photograph of the Camp Evans site in the WWII era was found in publicly accessible online collections. Wikimedia Commons has only modern (post-2000) exterior shots of the site. NARA RG111 likely holds aerial photos but they require a physical visit to the Still Pictures Research Room or a fee-based reproduction request. The Monmouth County Clerk's "Camp Evans Collection, 1954-1997" starts after the 1940s.
- **Project Diana staff group photo:** Available (5-man team photo from US Army Communications Electronics Museum) but copyright status unclear — it appears on multiple sites but always credited to the CECOM museum, not published to Commons.

## Recommendation for Edit

Use **Candidate 1** (Diana Radar moon-tower) as the primary 1940s beat image for Scene 22. It's the single most recognizable image from this specific site in this specific era, it directly illustrates the narration ("bounced radar off the moon"), and it's unambiguously public domain. The moonrise framing gives the editor a cinematic contrast: sepia wartime antenna reaching toward the moon → cut to modern InfoAge building under daylight sky.

If a second shot is needed for a montage, **Candidate 2** (daytime bedspring antenna) is a confirmed-PD cutaway. **Candidate 3** (SCR-268 truck, 1945) is the highest-res option but requires a license check before use.
