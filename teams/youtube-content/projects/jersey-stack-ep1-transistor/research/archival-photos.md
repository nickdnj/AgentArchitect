- Fair use / needs permission or verification: ~5 (notably the "Traitorous Eight" group portrait and any Computer History Museum / Nokia Bell Labs archive imagery).
- Gaps where no free photo was located (AI fill strongly recommended): Moe Abramson, Stanislaus (Stanley) Danko portraits; the actual Dec 23 1947 transistor demo moment; any contemporaneous (1947-49) Auto-Sembly production line at Fort Monmouth; Interdata founders / Oceanport-era corporate imagery; the specific PDP-11/20 with KS-11 MMU that Thompson and Ritchie used (we have the publicity shot of them WITH it, but not of the room / lab alone); Mervin Kelly in an on-the-job Bell Labs setting (the two free Kelly photos are magazine headshots).

## Critical geography reminder (embedded here so the script writer doesn't confuse them)

- **Bell Labs Murray Hill** (technically Berkeley Heights / New Providence, NJ — 600 Mountain Ave) — where Bardeen and Brattain demonstrated the first transistor on December 23, 1947. Still active today as Nokia Bell Labs. **This is the birthplace.**
- **Bell Labs Holmdel** (Eero Saarinen mirrored-glass box, opened 1962; now "Bell Works" post-2013 redevelopment) — built 15 years AFTER the transistor. Home of the **transistor-shaped water tower** (1961/62) which is a monument to the Murray Hill invention, not the site of it. Famous now as the Lumon Industries HQ in Apple TV+'s *Severance*. **This is the memorial / cultural-memory site.**

Every location photo below is tagged MURRAY HILL or HOLMDEL explicitly.
