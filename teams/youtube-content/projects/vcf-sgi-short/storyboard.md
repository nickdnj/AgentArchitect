## 1. Concept & Hook Rationale

This converts because it weaponizes one of the most-quoted lines in movie history ("It's a UNIX system — I know this!") as a 2-second hook, then delivers a satisfying myth-bust payoff: the interface everyone mocked as fake Hollywood was 100% real SGI software (`fsn`), and the same family of machine that *appeared* in Jurassic Park also *made* the dinosaurs. The "movie computer that helped make the movie" turn is a self-contained dopamine hit that begs to be shared. We then collapse the global story into a hyper-local punch — "this exact machine is sitting in Wall, New Jersey, and you've driven past the sign" — converting curiosity into a frictionless visit CTA. Motion (the spinning `fsn` demo + the movie clip) does the persuading; we never linger on a static hardware photo.

## 4. Full Narration Script (continuous VO for TTS / voiceover)

> *(Shot 1 is the movie's own audio — no VO. VO begins at ~0:03.)*

"For thirty years, people called this scene fake Hollywood nonsense.

It wasn't. That's `fsn` — real software SGI shipped on UNIX in 1993. Only the door-locking part was made up.

This is an SGI workstation, booting a 3-D desktop while PCs were still staring at a black DOS prompt.

Here's the twist: ILM built the dinosaurs on about seventy of these same SGI machines. The computer in the movie helped make the movie.

And ours still runs — the spinning demos, the three-button mouse, all of it. Live.

And it's not in California. It's in Wall, New Jersey — minutes from the Shore. You've probably driven right past the sign.

Come watch it run."

**Word count:** ~115 words → ~34s of VO at a relaxed pace, leaving the 3s cold-open clip and a 1s end-card beat. Fits 38s comfortably; trim "all of it. Live." if you need to hit 35s.

> **Honesty note for the creator (do NOT voice):** the film's exact hero machine was an SGI *Crimson*; ours is an *Indigo²* — same family, same year, same IRIX, same `fsn` software. The script says "these same SGI machines," which is accurate at the family level. If you want bulletproof accuracy, change Shot 5 text to "**~70 SGI machines like this one**." Optional on-screen fine-print in Shot 5: *"hero machine: SGI Crimson · ours: Indigo² — same family, same IRIX."*
