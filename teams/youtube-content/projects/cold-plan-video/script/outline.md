# Video Outline: NyQuil Costs $12. These 3 Pills Cost $0.47.

## Target: Consumer Advocacy / Explainer, 6-7 minutes, General YouTube adults

---

## Chapter 1: The Hook (0:00 - 0:45)
### Summary
A pattern-interrupt price reveal that stops the scroll. We show the shocking math up front -- NyQuil is $12 for ingredients that cost under fifty cents -- then tease that every brand-name cold medicine works the same way.
### Key Points
- NyQuil = $12 at the store. The same three active ingredients bought individually = $0.47 per dose.
- This is not a knockoff or a "hack" -- it is literally the same FDA-regulated chemicals at the same doses.
- Every brand-name cold medicine is built this way. We are about to prove it.
### Scenes: 3
### Visual Approach
Dramatic price comparison graphics. Warm illustrated NyQuil bottle next to three small pills. Big price numbers ($12 vs $0.47) with animated karaoke highlight. Cozy editorial illustration style. The opening visual should be immediately shareable.

---

## Chapter 2: The Reveal (0:45 - 1:50)
### Summary
Explain the combo-drug business model. Pharma companies take 2-3 generic drugs, combine them into one branded package, and charge a premium for convenience. We demystify this without conspiracy -- it is just how consumer products work. Introduce the "Kleenex name" concept: every generic drug has a brand name people already know.
### Key Points
- Brand cold medicines are "combo meals" -- bundled generics in one package at a markup
- The FDA requires the same active ingredients at the same doses, brand or generic
- The markup pays for the brand, the packaging, and the convenience of one pill instead of three
- Every generic has a "Kleenex name" (Acetaminophen = Tylenol, Ibuprofen = Advil, Dextromethorphan = Robitussin, Diphenhydramine = Benadryl)
### Scenes: 3
### Visual Approach
Illustrated "combo meal" metaphor -- pills going into a branded box like toppings on a burger. Split-screen brand vs. generic comparisons. Warm hand-drawn style with clean infographic elements for ingredient lists. The "Kleenex name" scene uses playful labels.

## Chapter 3: Brand Breakdowns (1:50 - 4:10)
### Summary
The meat of the video. We break down five popular brand-name cold medicines into their generic recipes, showing pill counts and per-dose cost comparisons. Each breakdown follows the same visual formula: brand package on one side, generic pills on the other, price comparison front and center. A quick aside about Pseudoephedrine being pharmacy-counter-only adds personality.
### Key Points
- NyQuil Cold & Flu = Acetaminophen 325mg + Dextromethorphan 15mg + Diphenhydramine 25mg = 3 pills, ~$0.12 vs $12-15
- DayQuil Cold & Flu = Acetaminophen 325mg + Phenylephrine 10mg + Dextromethorphan 15mg = 3 pills, ~$0.19 vs $12-15
- Advil Cold & Sinus = Ibuprofen 200mg + Pseudoephedrine 30mg = 2 pills, ~$0.12 vs $10-14
- Theraflu Nighttime = Acetaminophen 650mg + Phenylephrine 10mg + Diphenhydramine 25mg = 3 pills, ~$0.23 vs $10-12
- Advil PM = Ibuprofen 200mg + Diphenhydramine 25mg = 2 pills, ~$0.05 vs $10-14
- Pseudoephedrine fun fact: you cannot buy it on Amazon -- it is federally regulated under CMEA, pharmacy counter only
### Scenes: 7 (one per brand + pseudoephedrine aside)
### Visual Approach
Each brand gets its own "recipe card" scene -- illustrated brand box/bottle on one side, individual generic pills on the other, with price comparisons in large dramatic type. Karaoke highlights on the price numbers. Consistent visual formula creates a satisfying pattern. Pseudoephedrine aside gets a pharmacy counter illustration with a fun "behind the counter" visual.
