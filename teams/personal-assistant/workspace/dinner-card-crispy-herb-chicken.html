Tonight's Dinner · Serves 2

Crispy Lemon-Garlic-Thyme Drumsticks

with roasted garlic-thyme Yukon golds, lemony broccoli & a dip bar

Prep
15 min
Cook
45 min
Total
~1 hour
Heat
450°F
Serves
2

Ingredients

  • Chicken drumsticks1½ lb
  • Yukon gold potatoes1½ lb
  • Broccoli1 head
  • Garlic6 cloves
  • Shallot1
  • Fresh thyme6 sprigs
  • Lemon1
  • Butter4 Tbsp
  • Baking powder½ tsp
  • Baking soda½ tsp
  • Olive oil3 Tbsp
  • Salt & black pepperto taste
Pick up at the store
  • Baking powder + baking soda — the crispy secret
  • Butter (1 stick)

Instructions

1

Prep & preheat 0:00

Heat oven to 450°F. Pat the thawed chicken very dry with paper towels — dry skin = crispy skin. Mince 4 cloves garlic, finely dice the shallot, strip the leaves from 4 thyme sprigs. Zest the lemon, then cut it in half.

2

Potatoes in first 0:05

Cut potatoes into ¾" chunks. Toss with 2 Tbsp olive oil, 2 smashed garlic cloves, 2 thyme sprigs, salt & pepper. Spread on a sheet pan and roast 35–40 min, flipping once at the halfway mark.

3

Season & roast the chicken 0:10

Mix ½ tsp baking soda + ½ tsp baking powder with 1 tsp salt and pepper, then toss with the dried drumsticks until evenly coated. Arrange on a wire rack set over a foil-lined sheet pan. Roast 40–45 min until deep golden and crisp.

No rack? Roast straight on the pan and flip once — still great, just rotate for even crisping.
4

Roast the broccoli 0:30

Cut broccoli into florets. Toss with 1 Tbsp olive oil, 2 sliced garlic cloves, salt & pepper. Roast 18–20 min until edges char. Finish with a squeeze of fresh lemon.

5

Make the herb-lemon butter 0:40

In a small pan, melt 4 Tbsp butter with the minced garlic, diced shallot, thyme leaves, and lemon zest. Simmer 1–2 min until fragrant. Squeeze in half the lemon.

6

Toss, plate & serve 0:45

Add the hot, crispy drumsticks to a big bowl, pour over the herb-lemon butter, and toss to coat. Plate with the potatoes and broccoli. Dinner's served. 🍗

The Science · Why Baking Soda + Powder?

Crispy skin comes down to two things: dry and brown. The baking soda is alkaline — it raises the skin's pH, which speeds up the Maillard browning reaction so the skin colors and crisps before the meat dries out. It also pulls moisture to the surface, where the oven's heat evaporates it, leaving a thin, shatter-crisp shell instead of soft, rubbery skin. The baking powder adds a touch more lift and tempers the soda so there's no soapy edge. Together they give you fried-chicken crunch straight from the oven — no frying, no mess.
