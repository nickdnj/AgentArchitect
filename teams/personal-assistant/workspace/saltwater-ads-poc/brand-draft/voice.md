# Saltwater Brand Voice — Draft v1

**Source:** Joe DeMarco's email reply 2026-04-29 + visual review of ad library JD001-JD021.
**Status:** POC draft. Will be promoted to `context-buckets/saltwater-brand/files/voice.md` after POC validates.

## Voice IS

- Comfortable
- Coastal
- Elevated

## Voice IS NOT

- Try-hard
- Flashy
- Generic

## Vocabulary IN (use these)

comfort, premium, coastal, lightweight, breathable, effortless, versatile, soft, timeless, beach to boat, ready for anything, family business, built for comfort

## Vocabulary OUT (avoid)

cheap, tacky, hypebeast, gimmick, fratty, disposable, corny, stiff, stuffy

## Trademarks (use verbatim, never paraphrase)

- **Coastal Comfort™** — registered. Always with the ™ symbol. Never "coastal comfort" lowercase, never "the comfort of the coast", never substitute synonyms.

## Tone rules

- Confident without being aggressive
- Family business — relatable, not corporate
- Premium positioning, but never "luxury" language
- Direct, slightly defiant edge (see: "This Shirt Doesn't Suck")
- Every claim should pass the gut test: would a Long Island business owner in his 30s-40s say it without rolling his eyes?

## Founder voice

Joe DeMarco co-founded Saltwater with his brother Buddy. Family business is core to brand identity. The "Joe + Veronica Irish Flag Push" concept (JD013) is the proven founder-couple format. Founder ads should sound like Joe talking to a friend over a beer, not a marketer reading a script.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- AI-slop generic DTC copy ("Discover the difference", "Elevate your wardrobe", "Crafted for the modern man")
- Hypebeast/streetwear vocabulary
- Fratty bro voice
- Anything that sounds like an agency wrote it
- Generic comfort claims without specificity ("So comfortable!" → bad. "Soft enough you'll forget you're wearing it from morning gym to evening dinner" → on-brand.)
