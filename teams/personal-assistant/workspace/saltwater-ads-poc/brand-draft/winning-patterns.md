# Saltwater Winning Ad Patterns — Draft v1

**Source:** Joe DeMarco's email reply 2026-04-29 + visual review of ad library JD001-JD021.
**Status:** POC draft. Will be ground-truthed against Triple Whale Creative Cockpit data once API key arrives.

## Three patterns Joe identified as proven winners

### Pattern 1 — Founder Story

**Why it works (per Joe):** People connect with real people and family business authenticity.

**Confirmed example in library:**
- **JD013 "Joe + Veronica Irish Flag Push"** — Joe with his fiancée, Irish flag styling, founder-couple format

**Hook tone:**
- Conversational, first-person from a co-founder voice
- Reference family, the actual founding moment, why we started Saltwater
- Never corporate

**Example hook templates:**
- "My brother and I started Saltwater because we got tired of [specific pain]..."
- "Veronica and I were [specific moment] when..."
- "This is the shirt we made for [specific occasion]..."

### Pattern 2 — Problem / Solution

**Why it works (per Joe):** The pain point is immediate.

**Confirmed example in library:**
- **JD019 "This Shirt Doesn't Suck"** — review-collage format, real customer reviews around hero product shot
- Product: blue gingham performance button-down
- Real review quotes used verbatim: "Husband's favorite!", "10/10 Shirt", "Best fitting shirt", "Beyond Comfortable"

**Hook tone:**
- Direct, slightly defiant ("doesn't suck" is the brand's edge)
- Punchy headline
- Let real customer quotes carry the proof in body copy

**Example hook templates:**
- "Your old [item] is [problem]. This one isn't."
- "Stiff, hot, wrinkled? Not this one."
- "Your husband's favorite shirt — said by 600+ verified buyers"

### Pattern 3 — Limited Drops / Urgency

**Why it works (per Joe):** Timing + scarcity matters.

**Confirmed examples in library:**
- **JD006 "Coastal Comfort™ Valentines BOGO"** — couple shot, BOGO heart treatment, Valentines capsule
- **JD018 "USA Collection Shop Now"** — patriotic styling, Memorial Day timing, US flag motifs

**Hook tone:**
- Time-bounded and specific (real drops only — never fake urgency)
- Tie to real seasonal moment, not generic "limited time"

**Example hook templates:**
- "[Holiday] capsule — limited drop"
- "Almost sold out — only [N] left in [size/color]"
- "[Season] refresh dropped this week"

## Products that win

(From Joe's email + library evidence)
- Performance button-downs (JD018 dedicated set — multiple variants)
- Polos
- Quarter-zips / hoodies in season (JD014 Vintage Fleece Hoodie, JD015 Static Fleece)
- Bundles
- Everyday staples with clear comfort story

## Products that lose

(From Joe's email)
- Items needing too much explanation
- Basic products with no hook
- Wrong audience fit
- Items with weak perceived value

## Visual brand DNA (locked from ad library)

- **Format:** 9:16 vertical (Meta + IG + future TikTok native)
- **Photography:** Outdoor natural light ALWAYS — never studio
- **Locations:** Boat, dock, beach, lakeside, coastal scenes
- **Talent ensemble:** Joe, Veronica, Nick, Gina, Erin (recurring)
- **Typography:** Big bold sans-serif, punchy
- **Color palette:** Navy + white + USA red; seasonal pink for Valentines; natural earth tones
- **Brand mark:** Stylized shark+saltwater logo (used on hat patches, shirt graphics, and brand corner badges)
- **CTA pattern:** Single button, simple verbs ("SHOP NOW", "BOGO")
- **A/B discipline:** Every concept ships V1/V2/V3/V4 — sometimes V5/V6
- **Cadence:** ~22 concepts in 10 months (Jan-Nov 2025) ≈ 2 concepts/month
