# Sebco Audit & My Magic Pass App Research

**Date:** 2026-04-04
**Session type:** research
**Agents involved:** Wharfside Board Assistant, Email Research, Voice Mode, Web Research

## Summary

Giuseppe forwarded a Sebco marketing flyer for the My Magic Pass app and raised concerns about the lack of audit capability in the Sebco addendum. Researched the app's reporting features, Sebco's owner-facing tools, BBB profile, and industry best practices for laundry lease audit clauses. Drafted recommendation email to Giuseppe with proposed right-to-audit language.

## Key Findings

- **My Magic Pass app** is consumer-facing only — real-time availability, contactless payments, cycle notifications. No owner dashboard or reporting portal found publicly.
- **Sebco website** (sebco.com/for-owners/) mentions "Mobile Laundry Room Monitoring" but provides zero detail on reporting, dashboards, or owner data access.
- **MyMagicPass.com** is just a login/registration page — no owner portal visible.
- **Sebco BBB profile:** NOT accredited. Numerous complaints about broken machines, unresponsive service, and unfulfilled refunds. Described as "by FAR the worst laundry company" in reviews.
- **Addendum Clause 3** already requires Sebco to "print the collections on every quarterly check" — provides a basic paper trail but no right to verify.
- **Industry standard** (per CooperatorNews): laundry contracts should include explicit right-to-audit clauses allowing periodic examination of financial records.
- **Revenue share model:** Buildings typically receive >50% of collections, paid monthly or quarterly. The digital app creates a complete electronic audit trail — the data exists, the question is access.
- **Sebco revenue:** $20.9M annually (2025), primarily East Coast multi-housing.

## Decisions Made

- Recommend adding a right-to-audit clause to the addendum before signing
- Proposed language: "The Association shall have the right, upon 30 days written notice, to inspect or audit Sebco's records related to machine usage and collections at 120 Wharfside Drive, no more than once per calendar year."
- Framed as "trust but verify" per Giuseppe's guidance
