RESOLUTION NO. 2006-01

WHEREAS, Article VI, Section 1 of the By-Laws of Wharfside Manor Condominium Association, provides that "The affairs of the Association shall be governed by a Board of Trustees consisting of not less than five (5) members." and

WHEREAS, Article VI, Section 14 of the By-Laws for Wharfside Manor Condominium Association, provides that "The Board of Trustees shall have and exercise all lawful powers and duties necessary for the proper conduct and administration of the affairs of the Association and the operation and maintenance of the Condominium and may do or cause to be done all such other lawful acts and things as are not by law, by these By-Laws or otherwise, directed or required to be done or exercised by members of the Association or owner of units, or by others."; and

WHEREAS, it is the intent of the Board to adopt a Resolution to Memorialize a Code of Conduct and Ethics for the Board of Trustees.

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED THAT the following Code of Conduct and Ethics for the Board of Trustees be and is hereby adopted:

I FINANCIAL ETHICS:

  • No Board member will solicit or accept, directly or indirectly, any gifts, gratuity, favor, entertainment, loan or any other thing of monetary value from a person who is seeking to obtain contractual or other business or financial relations with the Association, or from any person whose intent it is to influence any decision or action on any official matter.
  • No Board member will use his/her position to enhance his/her financial or business position or undertaking.
  • No Board member or his/her agent or employee or family member will enter into a personal service contract with the Association without previous disclosure of such interest to the Board and Unit Owners.
  • Any Potential conflict of interest will be disclosed to the Board and Unit Owners.
  • No Board member will receive any compensation from the Association for acting as such.
  • No Board member will use his/her position to seek personal political advantage or contribute Association funds or favors to any political party candidate.

II BUSINESS ETHICS:

  • No Board member will seek to have a contract or policy implemented, cancelled or changed that has not been duly approved by the Board.
  • No promise of anything not approved by the Board as a whole will be made to any contractor, supplier, or contractor during negotiations, site inspections, meetings or discussions.
  • No Board member will interfere with the duties of any Association professional, managing agent, contractor, or staff member, nor give direction to any Association professional, managing agent, contractor, or staff unless duly authorized by the Board.
  • No Board member will harass, threaten, or attempt through any means to control or instill fear in any Association professional, managing agent, contractor, or staff.

  • No Board member will conduct meetings on behalf of the association or Board without the prior approval of the Board of Trustees prior to such meeting.
  • Each Board Member will abide by the requirements of the Association's Master Deed and By-Laws.
III ETHICAL RELATIONS AMONG PERSONS:
  • No Board member will engage in any writing, publishing, or speech making that defames any other member of the Board, Unit Owner, or resident of the Community.
  • Language at Board meetings will be kept professional. Personal attacks against owners, residents, officers, and trustees are not consistent with the best interest of the Community and are not permitted.
IV INFORMATION AND DISCLOSURE ETHICS:
  • The Board will disclose all information about its action(s) through open Association meetings and by making readily accessible Association business and financial reports as required by State Law, and Board meeting agendas and minutes.
  • No Board member will willingly misrepresent facts to the Unit Owners and residents, either by withholding, distorting, or fabricating information.
  • Claims of privacy and confidentiality will not be used to impede the proper flow of information to which Unit Owners are legally entitled.
IV ETHICAL RESOLUTION OF DIFFERENCES:
  • Differences of opinion about policy or procedures, among Board members or between Board members and unit Owners, will be discussed rationally, on their merits and in a timely and courteous manner, and with emphasis on resolving differences.
  • The Board will treat all members of the Community even-handedly and without favoritism.
  • When any dispute arises between the Board and a Board member and/or any Unit Owners, the Board will comply with due process procedures, and attempt to utilize Alternate Dispute Resolution when possible.
VI PRIVACY ETHICS:
  • No Board member will violate the confidentiality of a Unit Owner's file maintained by the Association.
VII ACCOUNTABILITY ETHICS:
  • Any Board member who is unable to fulfill adequately the duties of the position in a timely manner shall resign or request leave of absence.
  • Any Board member who is found to have violated this Code of Conduct and Ethics agrees that the Board of Trustees may seek injunctive relief against him/her and agrees to pay the attorney's fees incurred by the Association in that enforcement effort.
  • Any Board member under investigation for an indictable offense will take a leave of absence from the Board of Trustees during the investigation and trial period. Any Board member convicted of an indictable offense will voluntarily resign from his/her position.
  • This Code of Conduct and Ethics will be posted in an accessible location and will be made part of the Association Rules and Regulations that are distributed to all Unit Owners and residents.

RESOLUTION OF WHARFSIDE MANOR CONDOMINIUM ASSOCIATION
ADOPTING ALTERNATIVE DISPUTE RESOLUTION PROCEDURE

WHEREAS, N.J.S. 46:8B-14(k) requires Wharfside Manor Condominium Association (the "Association") to provide a fair and efficient procedure for the resolution of housing-related disputes between an Owner and the Association and between owners, as an alternative to litigation, and that a person other than an officer or trustee of the Association shall be available to address said dispute; and

WHEREAS, Article VI, Section 1 of the By-Laws of Wharfside Manor Condominium Association, provides that "The affairs of the Association shall be governed by a Board of Trustees consisting of not less than five (5) members."; and

WHEREAS, for the benefit of the Association and of the individual Owners, the Board deems it necessary and desirable to establish procedures for dispute resolution concerning housing-related disputes to attempt to minimize the necessity of judicial intervention; and

WHEREAS, the Board has the power to enforce the Association's Governing Documents on its own behalf and on behalf of all Owners; and

WHEREAS, the Governing Documents empowers the Association to impose reasonable fines for failure to comply with the Governing Documents so long as the owner(s) is given written notice of the action taken and the alleged basis therefore, and is advised of the right to participate in dispute resolution; and

WHEREAS, Article VI, Section 14 of the By-Laws for Wharfside Manor Condominium Association, provides that "The Board of Trustees shall have and exercise all lawful powers and duties necessary for the proper conduct and administration of the affairs of the Association and the operation and maintenance of the Condominium and may do or cause to be done all such other lawful acts and things as are not by law, by these By-Laws or otherwise, directed or required to be done or exercised by members of the Association or owner of units, or by others."; and

WHEREAS, in consideration of the mandates of New Jersey Law, the Board deems it necessary to adopt a uniform procedure to attempt to resolve disputes between Owners and the Association and between owners; and

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the following Alternative Dispute Resolution Procedure be and is hereby adopted:

A. An Alternative Dispute Resolution Committee ("Committee") is hereby created. A. The Committee shall provide a forum for resolution of housing related disputes between individual owners and the Association and between owners as an alternative to litigation. At least 10 days prior to any non-binding arbitration, the Committee shall serve a copy of the Complaint, or serve the details thereof which may be in the form of a letter (either document to be known as the "Notice of Non-Binding Arbitration"), upon the respondent by (1) personal service or (2) by registered or certified mail, return receipt requested and via regular mail, addressed to respondent at the respondent's last known address. Service by mail will be deemed effective, in the event proof of certified mailing is not secured, if the regular mailing is not returned. The Notice of Non-Binding Arbitration shall contain a statement that the respondent may be present at the non-binding arbitration; may, but need not be, represented by counsel; may present any relevant evidence; and be given full opportunity to cross-examine all witnesses and the production of books, documents, or other items by applying to the Board or managing agent.
  4. Amended or Supplemental Complaints. At any time prior to the non-binding arbitration date, the Committee may file or permit the filing of an Amended or Supplemental Complaint. All parties must be notified in the same manner as notified of the original Complaint, and, if necessary, a new non-binding arbitration date. If new charges are presented, the respondent may file a Supplemental Answer.
  5. Discovery. At any time after service of the Notice of Non-Binding Arbitration and prior to the date for non-binding arbitration, either party may request the Board or the Managing Agent to provide the names and addresses of witnesses to be called, and copies of any statements, writings, and investigative reports to be introduced at the non-binding arbitration. It shall be the obligation, however, of any individual to which such information is provided, to utilize same only for legitimate purposes in the context of the non-binding arbitration. Failure to do so shall subject the unit owner to liability and/or penalties.
  6. Non-Binding Arbitration. The Committee will select a person who need not be an owner or a member of the Committee to preside as non-binding arbitration officer. It is the duty of the non-binding arbitration officer to explain the rules and procedures by which the non-binding arbitration is to be conducted. Generally, any relevant evidence may be admitted and hearsay evidence may be used to supplement or explain other evidence, but will not be sufficient in itself to support a finding. Counsel for the Association may be present at the non-binding arbitration and may serve as a non-binding arbitration officer.

At the request of either complainant or respondent, or on its own motion, the Committee may conduct the non-binding arbitration in private session.

DOOR AND WINDOW INSTALLATION AND REPLACEMENT GUIDELINES

  1. At the Unit Owner's sole cost and expense, windows may be installed or replaced with a window subject to Board approval. The Board has pre-approved the window designs and models as detailed in Schedule A.

WHEREAS, the Board of Trustees recognizes that the Association’s Property has become negatively affected by feral cat populations and the feeding of those feral cats and other wild animals/birds on the Property; and

WHEREAS, the Board of Trustees desires to add a rule and regulation that governs the feeding of such non-domesticated animals and/or birds; and

WHEREAS, the Board also deems it desirable in the best interest of the Association to establish Rules and Regulations regarding the harboring of pets within the and insure the proper cleanup after pets to protect the common property from damage.

NOW, THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED THAT the following rules and regulations pertaining to animal control be and the same are hereby adopted:

  1. All dogs and cats must be licensed by the Borough of Monmouth Beach, Monmouth County, New Jersey.
  2. All dogs and cats must be on a leash while on the Common Property, and accompanied by their owner or caretaker. The leash may not be longer than 8 feet in length.
  3. No pet may be tied, leashed, staked, or otherwise affixed to the Common Property.
  4. No resident shall keep, harbor or maintain a pet which habitually disturbs the quiet enjoyment of neighbors, or is an annoyance or nuisance to the Community.
  5. Pet Owners shall be responsible for immediately cleaning up and proper disposal of their animal's waste regardless of location.
  6. No pet shall become a hazard or threat to any Unit Owner, Tenant, the General Public or member of the Association staff.
  7. Any complaints or problems related to pet noise will be addressed on an individual case basis. With regard to such incidents, owners are asked to first resolve problems on their own.
  8. No unit owner, resident and/or guest may feed any wildlife, which shall include, but are not limited to, “feral” cats and/or uncaged (wild) birds while on Common Property. A “feral cat is defined as the following:
    • a. An unowned and untamed cat separated from domestication, or
    • b. A domesticated cat that has returned to the wild, or the offspring of such a cat.

5-13.1 Purpose.
This section is enacted to prohibit the feeding of unconfined wildlife in any public park or on any other property owned or operated by the Borough so as to protect public health, safety and welfare and to prescribe penalties for failure to comply. (Ord. No. O-15-06 § I)

As used in this section:

Person shall mean any individual, corporation, company, partnership, firm, association, or political subdivision of this State subject to municipal jurisdiction.

Wildlife shall mean all animals that are neither human nor domesticated. (Ord. No. O-15-06 § II)

5-13.3 Prohibited Conduct.
No person shall feed, in any public park or on any other property owned or operated by the Borough of Monmouth Beach, any wildlife, excluding confined wildlife (for example, wildlife confined in zoos, parks or rehabilitation centers, or unconfined wildlife at environmental education centers).
