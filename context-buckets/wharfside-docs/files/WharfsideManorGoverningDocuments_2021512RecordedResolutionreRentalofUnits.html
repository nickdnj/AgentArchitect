WHARFSIDE MANOR CONDOMINIUM ASSOCIATION (“ASSOCIATION”)
RESOLUTION RELATING TO RENTAL OF UNITS
RESOLUTION NO. ____

WHEREAS, the Association was established and exists by virtue of the New Jersey Condominium Act, N.J.S.A. 46:8B-1 et seq. and by virtue of a certain Master Deed recorded April 18, 1983 in the Office of the Clerk of Monmouth County in Deed Book 4401, Page 587, et seq., as amended on April 10, 1984, recorded in Deed Book 4471, Page 543 et. seq. (the “Master Deed”);

WHEREAS, at the Association’s {November 30}, 2020 Board meeting a quorum of the Association’s Board of Trustees (“Board”) was present and the Board passed the Resolution to establish rules and regulations as it pertains to the rental of units within the Association;

WHEREAS, the Board has found it necessary to incorporate the authorities of the By-Laws and that Master Deed to adopt the following rules and regulations regarding rental of Units within the Association and have said rules and regulations recorded in the Monmouth County Clerk’s Office;

WHEREAS, Article IV, Section 9 of the Association’s By-Laws provides that “[a]ll Units shall be utilized for residential purposes only, and in accordance with the Master Deed and such other rules and regulations as the Association shall make unless otherwise specifically authorized in writing by the Board of Trustees”;

WHEREAS, Article IV, Section 11 of the Association’s By-Laws requires all members to comply with the By-Laws and “rules and regulations concerning the use of the units and common elements as may be amended from time to time”;

WHEREAS, Article IV, Section 1 of the Association’s By-Laws provides that “[t]he affairs of the Association shall be governed by a Board of Trustees…”;

WHEREAS, Article VI, Section 14 of the Association’s By-Laws provides that “[t]he Board of Trustees shall have and exercise all lawful powers and duties necessary for the proper conduct and administration of the affairs of the Association and the operation and maintenance of the Condominium and may do or cause to be done all such other lawful acts and things as are not by law, by these By-Laws or otherwise, directed or required to be done or exercised by members of the Association or owners of units, or by others”;

WHEREAS, Article VI, Section 14(Q) of the Association’s By-Laws provides that the Board of Trustees shall have the power “[t]o make and enforce compliance with such reasonable rules and regulations relative to the operation, use and occupancy of the Units, common elements and Association facilities, and to amend same from time to time, as when approved by appropriate resolutions, shall be binding on the owners and occupants of units, their successors in title and assigns”;

WHEREAS, Paragraph 19 the Association’s Rules and Regulations, provides, “[n]o unit shall be rented by the owners thereof for any rental for a period of less than 30 days or to any group other than a bona fide single housekeeping unit consisting of not more than the number of individuals permitted to live in that dwelling space as provided by the ordinances of the Borough of Monmouth Beach, County of Monmouth, State of New Jersey”;

WHEREAS, the Board has found it necessary to establish rules and regulations as it pertains to the rental of units within the Association to ensure the health, safety and welfare of the members; and

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED THAT the Board has found it necessary to incorporate the authorities of the By-Laws and that Master Deed to adopt the following rules and

regulations regarding rental of Units within the Association and said rules and regulations be and hereby adopted:

1. Paragraph 19 of the Association’s Rules and Regulations is hereby stricken and replaced with the following language:

19. No unit shall be rented by the owners thereof for any rental for a period of less than 6 months or to any group other than a bona fide single housekeeping unit consisting of not more than the number of individuals permitted to live in that dwelling space as provided by the ordinances of the Borough of Monmouth Beach, County of Monmouth, State of New Jersey.

2. Any unit owner intending to lease their dwelling unit shall notify the Association of said leasehold thirty (30) days prior to the commencement of the leasehold and provide the Association with a copy of the signed lease containing the noted provisions required by the Master Deed and By-Laws ten (10) days before the first day of the occupancy. The following Lease Rider attached hereto as Exhibit “B” must be utilized in connection with any lease agreement.

3. If a Unit Owner/landlord is in arrears of their monthly maintenance fees and/or fines and assessments, the Association can require the tenant to make monthly rent payments to the Association. The Association will apply the rent payment to the landlord’s outstanding maintenance/assessment balance for said unit. All surplus monies will be sent to the landlord. The Association will issue a receipt to the tenant for such rent payments made to the Association.

4. All tenants must abide by the Association’s rules and regulations.

5. All leases must be in writing and contain a provision that the terms of the lease are subject to the Governing Documents and rules and regulations of the Association and that

5

Page 8

failure by the lessee to comply with the terms of the Governing Documents and rules and regulations shall be deemed a default under the lease.

6. Ten (10) days before the first day of occupancy by the tenant, the Unit Owners must provide Management the following: a) a copy of the signed Lease Agreement and any and all renewals and/or changes to same; b) the Unit Owners updated information c) vehicle information of the new tenants; d) a copy of the Certificate of Occupancy; and e) written verification signed by the Unit Owner that their tenants has/have received a copy of the Governing Documents of the Association.

7. All tenant(s)’ vehicles must be registered with Management to be parked at the Association.

8. Each Unit Owner shall be responsible for the actions of their tenants.

9. Each Unit Owner shall comply with all Township Regulations. In the event the Board and/or Management has a reasonable belief that a Unit Owner is in violation of the Township Regulations, the Board and/or Management may investigate the suspected violation. If a violation is found, the Board and/or Management shall provide notice to the Unit Owner that a violation is found and same must be cured within fifteen (15) days.

10. Notwithstanding any of the above, the Association may exercise all rights and remedies available to it at law, in equity and/or pursuant to the Governing Documents of the Association.

11. If any Unit Owner fails to provide any of the required information and documentation to Management within fifteen (15) days of the occupancy of the Unit or violates any of the provisions in this Resolution or the Governing Documents of the Association

It is hereby agreed as follows:

  1. TENANTS DUTY TO COMPLY WITH THE PROVISIONS OF THE MASTER DEED, BY-LAWS AND RULES AND REGULATIONS FOR THE ASSOCIATION

    Tenant and Landlord acknowledge that this Lease is subject to all of the provisions of the Master Deed for the Association and the By-laws which govern the Association (collectively referred to as the “Governing Documents”), any Rules and Regulations promulgated thereunder including, but not limited to, any determination/decision made by the Board of Directors of the Association relating to the use and possession of the subject condominium unit as well as to the use of all common elements at the Association’s complex.

    Tenant and Landlord further acknowledge that any failure of the Tenant to fully comply with the terms and conditions of the Governing Documents and decisions shall constitute a material default under this Lease and be grounds for termination and eviction.

  2. LANDLORD’S APPOINTMENT OF CONDOMINIUM ASSOCIATION AS LANDLORD’S ATTORNEY-IN-FACT

    In the event the Tenant fails to comply with the provisions of the Governing Documents or any determination/decision made by the Board of Directors of the Association, then, in addition to all other remedies which it may have, the Association shall notify the Landlord of such violation(s) and demand that same be remedied through the Landlord’s efforts within thirty (30) days after such notice. If such violation(s) is not remedied within said thirty (30) day period, then the Landlord shall immediately thereafter, at his own cost and expense, institute and diligently prosecute an eviction action against the tenant on account of such violation(s). Such action shall not be compromised or settled without the prior written consent of the Association.

    In the event the Landlord fails to fulfill the foregoing obligation, then the Association shall have the right to institute and prosecute such eviction action as attorney-in-fact for the Landlord and at the Landlord’s sole cost and expense, including all attorneys’ fees and other costs incurred. Landlord acknowledges that said fees and costs shall be deemed to constitute a lien on his condominium unit and that collection thereof may be enforced

by the Association in the same manner as the Association is entitled to enforce collection of common expenses and assessments.

By execution of this Condominium Lease Rider, the Landlord does thereby automatically and irrevocably name, constitute, and appoint the Association as his/her attorney-in-fact for the purposes described herein.

3. PROHIBITION AGAINST ASSIGNMENT

The Tenant, without first securing prior written approval from the Association may not:

  • assign this lease;
  • sublet all or any part of the unit; and
  • permit any other person to use the unit.

4. ASSOCIATION’S RIGHT OF ACCESS TO UNIT

Tenant and Landlord recognize and acknowledge that the Association shall have access to the unit from time to time during reasonable hours as may be necessary for the maintenance, repair or replacement of any common elements(s) therein or accessible therefrom or for making emergency repairs necessary to prevent damage to common elements or to any other unit or units. No such notice shall be required for emergency situations.

5. LANDLORD’S ASSIGNMENT OF RENTS TO ASSOCIATION

Landlord herein acknowledges that the execution of this Lease and Condominium Lease Rider does not operate to relieve Landlord of his obligation to pay his unit’s proportionate share of the common expenses and assessments required by the Association. As security for the performance of Landlord’s continued obligation to pay his unit’s proportionate share of common expenses and assessments, Landlord herein assigns to the Association the rent payable to him by the Tenant.

Said assignment of rent shall, however, only become operative upon the Landlord’s failure to timely pay his unit’s proportionate share of common expenses and assessments after having been given thirty (30) days written notice by the Association as to his default. Upon Landlord’s failure to cure his default within said thirty (30) day period, the Association can, without having to institute any court action or proceeding, demand of Tenant that Tenant pay over to the Association any and all rent due Landlord under the terms of the lease.

Tenant herein covenants to fully cooperate with the Association and to remit rent directly to the Association if so demanded by the Association.

Rent received by the Association from the Tenant shall be applied as follows:

Second: To cure Landlord’s delinquency; and

Third: Excess rent to Landlord.

6. APPROVAL BY ASSOCIATION

This Lease and Condominium Lease Rider is subject to the approval of the Board of Directors of the Association. Any additions, deletions or corrections by the Landlord and each Tenant must be approved by the Association. When this Lease and Condominium Lease Rider have been signed by the Landlord and each Tenant, it must be submitted to the Association at least ten (10) days prior to the beginning of the rental term. Each time a new/different Tenant moves in a separate Lease, Condominium Lease Rider and Tenant Agreement Form must be submitted to the Board for review and approval.

7. OCCUPANCY LIMITATIONS

Landlord and Tenant herein expressly recognize and acknowledge that the occupants must comply with the municipal ordinances concerning the number of occupants for the Unit. Only one family is permitted to occupy a Unit at a time.

8. INSURANCE

All tenant(s) are strongly encouraged at all times to maintain renter’s insurance adequate to protect the personal property within the unit. The Association will not reimburse unit owner and/or his or her tenants for any and all expenses other than expenses for repairing the unit to builder’s grade.

9. DAMAGE TO PREMISES

If the demised premises, or any part thereof, shall be partially damaged by fire or other casualty not due to the negligence or willful act of the tenant(s), his or her employee, agent, family and/or visitor, the premises shall be promptly repaired by Landlord and there shall be an abatement of rent by the Landlord corresponding with the time during which, and the extent to which, the leased premises may have been uninhabitable; but if the leased premises should be damaged other than by the negligence or willful act of the tenant(s), his or her employee, agent, family and/or visitor to the extent that Landlord shall decide not to rebuild and/or repair, the term of the lease shall end and the rent shall be prorated up to the time of the damage. Tenant has an obligation to report any need for repairs to the Unit Owner. In the case of emergency situations, the Tenant should report any such emergency immediately to the Association.

10. ASSIGNMENT OF RIGHTS AND PRIVILEGES

Landlord assigns to Tenant the rights and privileges associated with Landlord's ownership of the Unit during the term of the Lease, provided that Tenant complies with the Master Deed, By-Laws and Rules and Regulations of the Association. This means that Tenant may use the common elements and facilities of the Association, subject to the rights of the Association to suspend all rights and privileges for violation of the Master Deed, By-Laws and Rules and Regulations of the Association and/or nonpayment of assessments by the Landlord. Landlord retains the right to cast all votes in Association matters.

We, as tenants, do hereby acknowledge receipt of a copy of the Association's Rules and Regulations. We do further acknowledge that we have had the opportunity to read the Association Rules and Regulations and agree to be bound by the same. In the event of any fines imposed due to not abiding by the Association Rules and Regulations, we agree to reimburse the Unit Owner for such fines.

Failure to fully comply with the terms and conditions of the governing documents of the Association shall constitute a material default under the lease and be grounds for lease termination and eviction.
